📋 What Happened — Background

March 19, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated strikes against Iran, codenamed Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The strikes killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei on the first day along with multiple senior Iranian officials, and targeted military infrastructure, nuclear facilities, and ballistic missile sites across nine Iranian cities. His son Mojtaba Khamenei was named successor on March 8.

By Day 20 (March 19), the conflict has escalated to include Iranian attacks on all six Gulf Cooperation Council nations (Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE) as well as Israel, Jordan, and Turkey. Israel yesterday killed security chief Ali Larijani and Basij commander Gholamreza Soleimani. In response to Israel striking Iran's South Pars gas field, Iran launched retaliatory strikes on Qatar's Ras Laffan terminal and a Saudi Aramco refinery, causing "extensive damage" and pushing oil prices to $116/barrel.

The death toll as of March 18 stands at over 1,444 killed in Iran, at least 21 in Israel (mostly civilians), 13 US service members, and nearly 1,000 in Lebanon where fighting with Hezbollah has resumed. Over 3.2 million Iranians and 816,000 Lebanese have been displaced.

✅ Arguments For the War

Military Objectives Advancing: Iranian ballistic missile launches have fallen by over 90% (from 350 on Feb 28 to ~25 by March 14). According to Israeli officials, 70% of Iran's ballistic missile launchers and over 150 air defense systems have been destroyed. The US now flies non-stealth B-1 bombers over Iranian airspace, signaling near-total air dominance.
Regime Leadership Decapitated: Over 48 senior Iranian figures have been eliminated, including Supreme Leader Khamenei, security chief Larijani (killed March 18), intelligence minister Khatib, and Basij commander Soleimani. Analysts note this has created "chaos" in IRGC command structures.
Nuclear Program Further Degraded: The Natanz and Fordow nuclear facilities remain damaged from strikes. Iran's defense industrial base is being systematically targeted to prevent reconstitution of enrichment capacity.
Regional Arab Support: A UN Security Council resolution condemning Iran's attacks on Gulf neighbors passed 13-0-2. UAE adviser Anwar Gargash stated Iran's attacks highlight "the folly of this Iranian strategy" and strengthen the US role in the region.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Massive Financial Cost: Operation Epic Fury cost US taxpayers $11.3 billion in munitions in just the first six days. The first 100 hours alone cost $3.7 billion. Pentagon expected to seek $50 billion in additional funding from Congress.
Critical Munitions Depletion: 168 Tomahawk cruise missiles ($3.6M each) were fired in just 100 hours—compared to only 322 produced in the prior five years. A Navy source said the service "will be feeling this expenditure for several years."
Global Economic Damage: Oil prices surged to $116/barrel (Brent). European gas prices jumped 24% after Iran struck Qatar's Ras Laffan terminal. California gas exceeded $5/gallon. The UN World Food Programme warned of significant long-term food price increases.
War Spreading Regionally: Iran has now attacked all six GCC states for the first time in history. Drones struck Azerbaijan, Saudi refineries, and Kuwait facilities. The conflict has spread to Lebanon with nearly 1,000 killed there.
Diplomatic Avenues Closed: The killing of Ali Larijani, who led nuclear talks before the war, may have been "aimed at closing the diplomatic avenues to end the war," according to analysts. Oman's Foreign Minister said "active and serious negotiations" had been "undermined."
Regime Change Unlikely Via Airpower: Stimson Center experts state: "No matter how precise or devastating, air strikes alone cannot topple a government." There is "no evidence of an uprising against the government" in Iran despite the strikes.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸US (Trump Admin): "The stated objectives remain the same: destroy the terrorist regime's ballistic missiles, raze their Iranian missile industry to the ground" — Press Secretary Leavitt
🇮🇱Israel: Expanded strikes to northern Iran for first time. Defense Minister announced elimination of Iranian intelligence minister. IDF struck over 7,600 targets.
🇬🇧UK: PM Starmer said he does "not believe in regime change from the skies" but allowed use of Diego Garcia and RAF Fairford bases for "specific and limited defensive purpose."
🇪🇺EU: Commission President von der Leyen called conflict "greatly concerning," reportedly supports "credible transition" in Iran. Deep internal divisions on response.
🇸🇦Saudi Arabia: Intercepted Iranian drones; warned it "reserved the right to take military actions" against Iran. Called strikes on Saudi airspace "cowardly."
🇶🇦Qatar: Expelled Iranian military and security attachés after "extensive damage" to Ras Laffan gas terminal. Part of 12-nation Arab statement calling on Iran to "immediately halt its attacks."
🇨🇳China: "China firmly opposes and strongly condemns" the strikes. Called it a "grave violation" of the UN Charter. Providing spare parts for missiles and radar technology to Iran.
🇷🇺Russia: Providing Iran with intelligence on US warship/aircraft positions per Washington Post. Putin called Khamenei killing a "cynical violation of all norms of human morals."
🇳🇴Norway: Foreign Minister Eide said strikes "not in line with international law. Preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat."
🇺🇸Joe Kent (resigned): Former Trump counterterrorism chief resigned, saying "We started this war due to pressure from Israel and its powerful American lobby" and there was "no intelligence" of an imminent threat.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Regime Change Viability: Can airpower alone topple the Iranian government? Stimson Center experts say "it has never by itself toppled a government" and there is "no comparable [indigenous rebel] force" in Iran today. But Israeli sources suggest the regime is "brittle" and could collapse "with one more push."

Duration and Escalation: Trump said he expected a "four-week" operation but conflict is widening. 71% of Americans expect it to last months or longer. Will ground troops become necessary? 74% of US voters oppose this.

Russia/China Response: Will Moscow and Beijing escalate beyond intelligence sharing and spare parts? Analysts say they "cannot afford to let Tehran fall" but are constrained by Ukraine war and economic priorities.

Post-War Iran: Will a successor regime be more moderate or more hardline? Wikistrat analysts warn "the worst-case scenario for the West is not chaos but a capable leader who pursues the same strategic goals more competently."

Nuclear Proliferation: Russian FM Lavrov warned the war could lead Iran—or Arab states—to pursue nuclear weapons: "The US doesn't attack those who have nuclear bombs."
