📋 What Happened — Background

March 26, 2026

The strikes began on 28 February 2026, when Israel and the United States launched attacks on targets across Iran, codenamed Operation Roaring Lion in Israel and Operation Epic Fury in the United States. Khamenei was killed in the first wave of strikes on February 28, as were dozens of other top Iranian officials. Just before the strikes began, on 27 February 2026, Oman's Foreign Minister Badr Al-Busaidi said a "breakthrough" had been reached in diplomatic negotiations, but after the US and Israel attacked Iran, Al-Busaidi said that he was dismayed and that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined.

Since the operation began, approximately 5,700 combat sorties have been flown, with more than 540 strike waves conducted across central and western Iran and dozens more deep inside Iranian territory. Iran's death toll in the war has surpassed 1,500, according to its Ministry of Health, while 15 people have been killed in Israel by Iranian attacks. Approximately 2,000 vessels and 20,000 seafarers are stranded in the Strait of Hormuz, creating severe logistical and humanitarian challenges.

✅ Arguments For the War

Degradation of Iranian military capacity: Israeli officials assess Iran currently lacks meaningful reconstitution capacity for missiles, with production capacity now zero, down from roughly 100 ballistic missiles per month before the war. Roughly 80 to 85 percent of Iran's air-defense architecture — including radars, interceptors, and detection systems — has been destroyed.
Nuclear program setback: The current campaign has damaged further the Natanz nuclear facility, the one in Fordow remains inoperable, and defence industrial facilities needed to reconstitute enrichment capacity are being systematically targeted.
Disruption of command structures: According to Israeli assessments, repeated attacks on the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps have severely disrupted command-and-control functions, with field commanders increasingly operating without central coordination.
Strategic positioning for regional allies: Iran's escalation against its Arab neighbors has aligned these countries more closely with Israel, mostly on a covert basis, and there is ample reason to believe this campaign would strengthen ongoing intelligence and security cooperation between Israel and its neighbors.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Massive civilian casualties: On 7 March, the Iranian Red Crescent Society reported that over 6,668 civilian units were targeted by US-Israeli strikes, including 5,535 residential units, 1,041 commercial units, 14 medical centres, and 65 schools. UN human rights experts characterized the strike on the school as a potential war crime under the Rome Statute.
Regional escalation and spillover: Conflict between Hezbollah and Israel escalated into the 2026 Lebanon war. The Lebanese Ministry of Public Health reports that at least 1,072 people have been killed and 2,966 wounded in Lebanon since the offensive escalated on March 2.
Global economic disruption: Fatih Birol, the head of the International Energy Agency, warned that the situation in the Middle East is "very severe" and is worse than the two energy crises of the 1970s put together. The White House is facing pressure to reach a deal as American voters express growing concern over rising prices and interest rates.
Uncertainty about post-war stability: Given that the Islamic Republic is more likely to continue fighting than to concede, the war's trajectory may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated.
Diplomatic failure and escalation risk: There are legitimate questions about whether Washington walked away too soon from diplomatic alternatives that showed real progress. Trump had given Iran a 48-hour ultimatum to open the strait to all ships, threatening otherwise to "obliterate" Iran's power plants, and Tehran said it would completely close the strait in retaliation.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

European - Mixed/Cautious
Critical of both sides
Norway's Foreign Minister said the attack is "not in line with international law" as "preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat." Spain denied the US the use of military bases for attacks on Iran and condemned the US-Israel war as a "big error." The UK, France, and Germany condemned Iranian counter-strikes but the Prime Minister said he did "not believe in regime change from the skies."
Middle East - Divided
Mostly anti-Iranian, pro-Israeli
Most Middle East countries criticized the Iranian attacks. Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries are insisting on being represented in any peace talks, demanding guarantees for the free flow of energy and the cessation of threats from Iranian missiles, its nuclear programme, and regional proxies.
Asia/Global - Call for Peace
Peace advocacy
China's top diplomat told Iran that "talking is always better than fighting", while French President Emmanuel Macron urged Tehran to engage in good-faith negotiations to end the war. In Asia, many condemned the US-Israeli attacks or called for peace.
UN/International Bodies
Concerns over civilian impact
The ICRC expressed concerns about the situation in Iran, with the organization's president saying the military escalation could lead to "devastating consequences for civilians." The International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) said that there was no evidence of a structured nuclear weapons program at the time of the strikes.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Iran's Foreign Ministry stated the military has lost control over several units, that are operating according to old general instructions. Trump told Axios he considered Mojtaba Khamenei "unacceptable" as Iran's leader, but Iran's cleric-led Assembly of Experts announced they had appointed Mojtaba Khamenei as supreme leader. The Islamic Republic is more likely to continue fighting than to concede, meaning the war's trajectory may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated.
