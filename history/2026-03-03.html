📋 What Happened — Background

February 28, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated military strikes on Iran, codenamed Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The operation targeted Iran's leadership, military installations, and nuclear facilities, resulting in the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. The conflict has now entered its fourth day as of March 3, 2026.

As of Day 4, the death toll in Iran has risen to approximately 787 people according to Iranian Red Crescent figures, including a controversial reported strike on an elementary girls' school in Minab that allegedly killed over 148 students. US casualties have risen to 6 service members killed and 18 wounded, while at least 11 have died in Israel and 8 in Gulf states from Iranian retaliatory strikes.

On March 3, Israel launched a ground invasion of Lebanon in response to Hezbollah attacks on northern Israel, opening a new front. The IDF struck Iran's state broadcaster (IRIB) headquarters, parliament building, and bombed the Assembly of Experts meeting to elect a new supreme leader. Iran announced closure of the Strait of Hormuz, threatening to "set ablaze" any vessel attempting passage. Iranian attacks hit the US Embassy in Riyadh, energy infrastructure across the Gulf, and Qatar has halted LNG production after its facilities were struck.

President Trump predicted the war could last "four to five weeks" but said operations are "substantially ahead of schedule." The stated US objective is to destroy Iran's nuclear and missile capabilities and encourage regime change, though Defense Secretary Hegseth stated the US goal "was not regime change."

✅ Arguments For the War

✓Eliminating Nuclear Threat: Netanyahu claims Iran's nuclear bunkers would have become "immune within months." Supporters argue this was a narrow window to prevent nuclear weapons capability.
✓Decapitation of Hostile Leadership: The killing of Khamenei, IRGC commanders, and nuclear scientists removes leadership that funded regional proxy groups and supported Russia's war in Ukraine with Shahed drones.
✓Degrading Iranian Military: The US reports destroying Iran's air defenses, sinking warships including the IRIS Jamaran, and significantly damaging ballistic missile capabilities, reducing immediate threats to Israel and Gulf allies.
✓Supporting Iranian Protesters: Trump administration argues this fulfills promises to Iranians who protested in January 2026 when thousands were killed by the regime. Some Iranian diaspora have celebrated in cities worldwide.
✓Tactical Military Success: Analysts note "tactical execution has been excellent" with US achieving near-complete air superiority over Iran.

❌ Arguments Against the War

✗Regional Escalation: Conflict has spread to 12+ countries including Lebanon (new ground war), Cyprus (UK base struck), and Gulf states. Hezbollah has re-entered combat, opening a northern front.
✗Civilian Casualties: UNESCO condemned the alleged school strike as "a grave violation of humanitarian law." Iran reports 787+ dead including disputed claims of 148 children at Minab school.
✗Economic Disruption: Strait of Hormuz effectively closed; oil up ~13%, natural gas up 40-50% in Europe/Asia. Maersk, Hapag-Lloyd, and other shippers suspended Gulf transits. Qatar halted LNG production.
✗Historical Precedent Against Airpower Success: Stimson Center experts note "air strikes alone cannot topple a government" and cite a century of evidence that bombing campaigns produce "solidarity" not "rebellion."
✗Legal and Constitutional Concerns: European Council on Foreign Relations calls strikes "an illegal war of choice." Norway's Foreign Minister said the attack "is not in line with international law." Critics note Trump bypassed Congressional authorization.
✗No Clear Post-Regime Plan: Atlantic Council notes Trump "did not outline an imminent threat from Iran, nor a detailed plan for what comes next." IRGC with 190,000 members remains intact as an institution.
✗Diplomatic Talks Abandoned: Oman's mediator said a "breakthrough" had been reached just before strikes began, with Iran agreeing to zero stockpiling and full IAEA verification.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Will the IRGC and regime institutions collapse or consolidate? Experts are divided. Some argue decapitation will lead to capitulation; others note the IRGC has 190,000 members and "won't go quietly." Atlantic Council: "Neither protests nor airstrikes alone are likely to end the regime's grip on power."

How long will the Strait of Hormuz remain closed? While Iran threatens to "set ablaze" any ships, analysts debate whether Iran can sustain a blockade. Energy Aspects: "The US and Israel would just take that out, very, very quickly."

Will this produce regime change or nationalist backlash? Stimson Center warns that bombing historically produces "solidarity" not rebellion. Some Iranians are rallying behind the government after attacks.

What is the actual US objective? Mixed messaging continues—Trump calls for regime change while Hegseth says "the US goal was not regime change."

Will European allies be drawn deeper into the conflict? UK already allowing use of bases; France and UK bases hit by Iranian drones/missiles.
