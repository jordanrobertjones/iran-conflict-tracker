📋 What Happened — Background

March 14, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States launched Operation Epic Fury and Israel launched Operation Roaring Lion—a joint military campaign against Iran. The opening strikes killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior officials. Mojtaba Khamenei, his son, was appointed as the new supreme leader on March 8, 2026.

As of Day 14 (March 14, 2026), the US has attacked over 6,000 targets in Iran. Secretary Hegseth reported Iranian drone assaults are down 95% and missile volume is down 90%. The US is deploying 2,200 Marines from Okinawa to the region. Trump announced the US "totally obliterated every military target" on Kharg Island, which handles 90% of Iran's oil exports.

Iran's Health Ministry reports at least 1,444 killed and 18,551 injured in US-Israel attacks since February 28. An estimated 3.2 million Iranians have been displaced. Defense Secretary Hegseth claims the new supreme leader Mojtaba Khamenei is "wounded and likely disfigured." Iran continues retaliatory strikes across the Gulf region and has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, sending oil prices over $100/barrel.

✅ Arguments For the War

✓
Degrading Iranian Military Capacity: US officials report Iran's ballistic missile launch rate has fallen 90-92% and drone launch rate is down 95%. Over 160-190 missile launchers have been destroyed, with 200 more disabled. Iran's navy, air force, and air defenses have been largely neutralized.
✓
Nuclear Program Setback: Strikes have targeted what remains of Iran's nuclear infrastructure following the June 2025 twelve-day war. Israeli PM Netanyahu stated Israel aims to prevent Iran from moving nuclear projects underground.
✓
Regime Destabilization: The assassination of Khamenei and dozens of senior officials has thrown Iran into political uncertainty. An IDI survey found 82% of Israeli public supports ongoing military operations. Some Iranian diaspora and opposition figures support regime change.
✓
International Support: NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte called Europe "supportive" of US attacks, saying Iran is a "threat." France authorized US use of French bases. UK PM Starmer allowed British bases for "defensive" strikes. Argentina's President Milei strongly supports the operation.

❌ Arguments Against the War

✗
Civilian Casualties: Iran's Health Ministry reports 1,444 killed and 18,551 injured since February 28, including victims aged 8 months to 88 years. A strike on a girls' school in Minab killed approximately 170 people. 3.2 million have been displaced.
✗
Global Economic Disruption: Oil prices have risen over 40% (to $100+/barrel). The Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed. US gas prices hit a 22-month high at $3.63/gallon. The IEA released a record 400 million barrels to calm markets. Qatar's energy minister warned the crisis "will bring down economies of the world."
✗
US Casualties & Costs: At least 13 US service members killed; approximately 200 wounded. Operation costs estimated at $900 million per day, with $11.3 billion spent in first six days. Six crew members died in a KC-135 crash in Iraq on March 13.
✗
Regional Escalation: Iranian strikes have hit Israel (including cluster munitions), UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, Oman, and even Turkey. Lebanon-based Hezbollah has reopened a northern front against Israel. A French soldier was killed by an Iranian drone in Iraqi Kurdistan.
✗
Cultural Heritage Destruction: UNESCO World Heritage Sites damaged include Golestan Palace, Naqsh-e Jahan Square, Chehel Sotoun, Ali Qapu, the Shah Mosque, and Falak-ol-Aflak fortress. UNESCO urged protection for Iran's heritage sites on March 11.
✗
Rally-Around-Flag Effect: Analysts note some Iranians who opposed the Islamic Republic have "recoiled at the prolonged and violent nature of the war." An Iranian sociologist reported a rally-around-the-flag effect. New supreme leader successfully installed despite opposition.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States: Congress rejected War Powers resolutions (Senate 47-53, House 212-219). Sen. Graham: "generational impact." Over 250 US organizations signed letter opposing war funding. Senate Democrats demand public oversight hearings.
🇮🇱 Israel: 82% public support per IDI survey. PM Netanyahu: aims to "create conditions for regime change." Defense Minister Katz disclosed Israel had planned strikes for mid-2026.
🇬🇧 UK: PM Starmer allowed bases for "defensive" strikes but stated he doesn't "believe in regime change from the skies." UK not participating militarily in strikes. RAF Akrotiri struck by Iranian drone.
🇫🇷 France: Authorized US use of French bases. Called for Iran to "immediately cease strikes." First French soldier killed by Iranian drone in Iraqi Kurdistan. Macron opposed lifting Russia sanctions over oil crisis.
🇪🇺 European Union: Called conflict "greatly concerning," urging restraint and de-escalation. Von der Leyen supports "credible transition" of power in Iran. EU calling for diplomatic initiative.
🇪🇸 Spain: PM Sánchez called war a "big error" and denied US use of military bases at Rota and Morón. Trump threatened trade embargo in response.
🇷🇺 Russia: FM Lavrov condemned strikes as "reckless" and "unprovoked act of armed aggression." Putin expressed condolences for Khamenei, called for "immediate cessation of hostilities."
🇨🇦 Canada: PM Carney: "supports the United States acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon" while calling for civilian protection and diplomacy. Not militarily engaged.
🇨🇳 China/Global South: Many Asian nations condemned US-Israeli attacks or called for peace. Indonesia paused engagement with US-initiated Board of Peace. Brazil, Mexico called for restraint.
Gulf States (GCC): Ordered Iran to cease attacks. UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait all facing Iranian retaliation. Ukraine deploying anti-drone teams to Gulf states.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

War Duration & Endgame: Trump has given mixed signals, calling it a "short-term excursion" but also saying it won't end until Iran has no weapons capacity. Sen. Graham: "I don't see this conflict ending today." Military planners reportedly tell Trump it requires "weeks at least" to complete objectives.

Nuclear Stockpile: Rep. Bill Foster raised concern that the administration "never had a plan for that nuclear stockpile of enriched uranium—to destroy it, to seize it, or to put it under international inspection." Some experts believe stockpile is buried underground.

Post-War Iran: RAND experts warn the Islamic Republic "will be fundamentally altered" but may not collapse. Analysts note Mojtaba Khamenei may pursue a more extreme course than his father, potentially including nuclear weapons development if Iran survives the conflict.

Iran's Economic War: CSIS analysts argue Iran is fighting a different war—"one aimed at the global economy." Strait closure and attacks on Gulf infrastructure are designed to pressure the US to halt operations.
