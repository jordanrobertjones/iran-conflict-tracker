📋 What Happened — Background

March 12, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States (Operation Epic Fury) and Israel (Operation Roaring Lion) launched a joint military campaign against Iran, beginning with nearly 900 strikes in the first 12 hours. The opening salvo killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and triggered massive Iranian retaliation across the Middle East.

As of Day 12 (March 12, 2026), the conflict has intensified significantly. Iran claims more than 1,300 civilians killed and nearly 10,000 civilian sites bombed. U.S. forces have struck over 5,000 targets in Iran. Eight U.S. service members have been killed and approximately 140 wounded. Mojtaba Khamenei was elected as the new supreme leader on March 8.

The conflict has spread across the region: Iran has launched strikes against Israel, and at least 9 other countries hosting U.S. forces including the UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Oman, Iraq, and Turkey (accidentally). Lebanon has seen renewed Israeli attacks on Hezbollah, with over 634 killed and 800,000 displaced. Iran has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, hitting multiple commercial vessels and threatening global oil supplies. Brent crude has surged past $100/barrel.

Iran has told mediators it will only agree to a ceasefire if the U.S. guarantees no future attacks—a demand unlikely to be accepted. Russia is reportedly providing Iran specific drone tactical advice. China, Russia, and France have contacted Iran regarding a potential ceasefire, but no diplomatic progress has been made.

✅ Arguments For the War

Degraded Iranian Military Capacity: U.S. says Iran's ballistic missile attacks have fallen 90% and drone attacks 83% since Day 1. The IDF reports neutralizing approximately 300 Iranian missile launchers. The U.S. has reportedly destroyed 17 Iranian warships including one submarine.
Leadership Decapitation: Supreme Leader Khamenei and dozens of senior officials were killed. Defense minister Aziz Nasirzadeh and IRGC commander Mohammad Pakpour reportedly killed. Multiple government institutions destroyed including Supreme National Security Council headquarters.
Iran's Weakened Regional Position: Lebanon has banned Hezbollah's military activities. Iran's counter-strikes on Arab Gulf states may leave it further isolated from regional relationships it had rebuilt. Hezbollah described as "significantly weakened."
Coalition Support: France authorized U.S. forces to use French bases. UK allowed U.S. use of Diego Garcia and RAF Fairford for defensive purposes. Canada expressed support for preventing Iran's nuclear weapon development. Argentina's President Milei has been the most vocal supporter in Latin America.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Civilian Casualties and Legal Concerns: Iran reports 1,300+ civilian deaths. A U.S. strike on a girls' school in Minab killed ~170 people; preliminary U.S. investigation found the U.S. was "likely" responsible with outdated targeting data. UN human rights experts characterized the strike as a potential war crime. WHO warned of toxic "black rain" from strikes on fuel depots.
Global Economic Disruption: Iran has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz. Oil prices surged 40%+ to above $110/barrel. IEA releasing record 400 million barrels from reserves. Qatar's energy minister warned continued conflict could "bring down economies of the world." Dow Jones fell 400+ points. Global food price increases warned by UN World Food Programme.
Regional Escalation: Iran has struck targets across 9+ countries. 800,000+ displaced in Lebanon. Dubai airport damaged. AWS data centers in UAE struck causing regional outages. Azerbaijan hit by Iranian drones, withdrew diplomats. UK RAF base in Cyprus struck. 20,000+ Americans evacuated from the region.
No Diplomatic Off-Ramp: Analysts warn the "decapitation" strategy has eliminated diplomatic off-ramps that existed in the 2025 12-Day War. ACLED assesses the conflict "may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated." Iran has ruled out immediate ceasefire, demanding guarantees of no future attacks. Sen. Chris Murphy called war plans "incoherent and incomplete."
International Law Questions: The attacks are "widely considered illegal under international law" according to legal scholars, violating the UN Charter's prohibition on force without Security Council authorization. UK PM Starmer stated he does "not believe in regime change from the skies." Spain withdrew its ambassador to Israel and denied use of military bases.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

United States: Trump demands Iran's "unconditional surrender"; says war will end "soon" when he decides. Secretary Hegseth says U.S. and Israel are "winning."
Israel: Netanyahu claims this will "usher in an era of peace" and create conditions for Iranian democracy. IDF conducting continued waves of strikes on Tehran and Beirut.
Argentina: President Milei commended operation, called Khamenei "one of the most evil, violent, and cruel individuals that human history has ever seen."
Saudi Arabia & UAE: Both signaled willingness to respond directly to Iranian attacks. Saudi Arabia stated it "will take all necessary measures to defend its security."
UK/France/Germany: Joint statement condemned Iranian counter-strikes but called for diplomacy. PM Starmer: "I do not believe in regime change from the skies." UK not participating militarily but allowing limited defensive use of bases.
Spain: Withdrew ambassador to Israel. Denied use of military bases for U.S. operations, prompting Trump's threat of economic retaliation.
UN Human Rights: High Commissioner Volker Türk called for "immediate cessation of hostilities." Special Rapporteur Ben Saul stated there is no legal basis for self-defence claim.
Qatar: Foreign Minister called for de-escalation; urged Iran and U.S. to return to negotiating table. Called Iran's attacks on neighbors bringing "benefit for no one."
Russia: Reportedly providing Iran specific tactical drone advice. Envoy met with U.S. Special Envoy Witkoff in Florida.
China: Contacted Iran regarding ceasefire according to Iranian officials. Analysis notes Beijing's significant role in the conflict's dynamics.
Ukraine: Deployed anti-drone expert teams to Qatar, UAE, and Saudi Arabia at their request to help counter Iranian drone attacks.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Duration and Endgame: Trump says war will end "soon" but also said "we haven't won enough." Analysts outline three scenarios from concluded campaign to prolonged conflict. Iran prepared for "long-term war of attrition."

Ground Troops: Trump mentioned possible use of ground troops; experts say they would be required for full regime change goals. Recovering Iran's underground uranium stockpile would require significant ground forces per current/former officials.

Ceasefire Prospects: China, Russia, and France have contacted Iran regarding ceasefire. Iran demands U.S. guarantee of no future attacks—unlikely to be accepted. No active negotiations underway.

New Supreme Leader: Mojtaba Khamenei was injured (fractured foot) in initial strikes and has not appeared publicly. His effectiveness and control remain uncertain.

Strait of Hormuz: Iran laying mines and attacking vessels. U.S. destroyed 16 Iranian minelayers. G7 discussing ship escorts. Remains critical flashpoint for global economy.
