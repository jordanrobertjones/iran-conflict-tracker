📋 What Happened — Background

March 25, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated strikes on Iran—codenamed "Operation Epic Fury" by the Pentagon and "Operation Roaring Lion" by Israel. The opening salvo killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior officials. Mojtaba Khamenei was elected as the new supreme leader on March 8, 2026. The conflict, now in its 26th day, has expanded across the Middle East, with Iran retaliating against Israel and Gulf states including Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, and Bahrain. The war has caused major disruptions to global shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, with oil prices surging above $114 per barrel.

Prior to the strikes, Omani-mediated negotiations had reportedly made significant progress, with Foreign Minister Badr Al-Busaidi stating on February 27 that a "breakthrough" had been reached on Iran's nuclear program. Critics have accused the US and Israel of undermining active diplomacy. Conflicting claims about peace talks emerged on March 23-25, with Trump claiming "productive conversations" while Iran denied any negotiations were taking place.

Casualties as of March 25: At least 1,500 killed and 18,551 injured in Iran; 15-18 killed in Israel; 13 US service members killed in combat; 22+ killed across Gulf states; over 1,070 killed in Lebanon since March 2; and 82+ killed in Iraq.

✅ Arguments For the War

⚡
Significant military degradation

Iranian ballistic missile launches have fallen by more than 90% (from 350 on Feb 28 to ~25 by March 14). According to some reports, 80% of Iran's capacity to strike Israel has been eliminated. Over 50 Iranian naval vessels destroyed, and US flying non-stealth B-1 bombers over Iran signals near-total air dominance.

☢️
Nuclear facilities damaged

The Natanz nuclear facility has been further damaged and Fordow remains inoperable. Defense industrial facilities needed to reconstitute enrichment capacity are being systematically targeted.

🎯
Regime leadership eliminated

Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior officials were killed. Ali Larijani (Secretary of Supreme National Security Council) killed on March 17. The IRGC's command and control structure has been significantly degraded.

🕊️
Some regional support

Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman vowed to employ military force against Iranian incursions. Argentina, North Macedonia, and several Gulf states have expressed support for the operations against Iran.

❌ Arguments Against the War

💀
Significant civilian casualties

Over 1,500 killed and 18,500+ injured in Iran. A strike on a girls' school in Minab killed approximately 170 civilians. Iranian Red Crescent reported 5,535 residential units, 65 schools, and 14 medical centers targeted. UN human rights experts characterized some strikes as potential war crimes.

🌍
Regional war expansion

Iran has struck nine countries: Israel, Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, UAE, Jordan, Saudi Arabia, and British base in Cyprus. Over 1,070 killed in Lebanon since March 2. Iraq has become a "secondary battleground." Lebanon declared Iranian ambassador persona non grata.

💰
Global economic disruption

Oil prices hit $114/barrel—highest since COVID. Strait of Hormuz partially blockaded. IEA chief warned situation is "worse than the two energy crises of the 1970s put together." Sri Lanka ordered energy rationing; Philippines declared national energy emergency. Stock markets have experienced significant declines.

⚖️
Legal and diplomatic concerns

War launched without congressional authorization. Norway's FM stated strikes are "not in line with international law." Spain denied US use of bases. Oman's mediator accused US of abandoning diplomacy that was "making progress." Only 25% of Americans support the war according to Reuters/IPSOS poll.

❓
Unclear endgame and goals

Analysts note shifting war aims from nuclear program to missile capabilities to regime change. Former UK official warned of potential "Iraq situation all over again." Brookings VP stated operations are "more complicated than the White House may have hoped." Carnegie analyst noted Iran has "10 out of 10 resolve" as regime survival is existential.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 US (Trump Administration)

President Trump paused planned strikes on Iranian power grid for 5 days on March 23, claiming "productive conversations." White House cautioned situation is "fluid." Republican leaders largely supportive; some Republicans (Massie, Paul) and Democrats (Schumer, Kaine) opposed, calling for congressional authorization.

🇮🇱 Israel (Netanyahu Government)

Continuing operations independently. UN Ambassador Danon stated Israel is "not part of US-Iran talks" and military operations will continue until Iran's nuclear and missile capabilities are eliminated. Warned of potential ground invasion of Lebanon south of Litani River.

🇮🇷 Iran

Denies any negotiations with US despite Trump's claims. New Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei installed March 8. Iran executing "multidomain punishment campaign" across energy, cyber, and maritime systems. Stated "non-hostile vessels" can transit Hormuz.

🇴🇲 Oman

Lead mediator FM Al-Busaidi said peace was "within reach" before strikes. Wrote in The Economist that US has "lost control of its own foreign policy" and accused Israel of persuading Trump into a "grave miscalculation" and "catastrophe."

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

PM Starmer allowed use of British bases for "defensive" strikes. Called emergency meeting over economic fallout. Sending short-range air defense systems to Gulf. RAF Akrotiri base in Cyprus struck by Iranian drone.

🇫🇷 France

Macron urged Tehran to engage in "good-faith negotiations" and demanded Iran end "unacceptable attacks" on neighbors. Called for restoring freedom of navigation in Strait of Hormuz.

🇪🇸 Spain

Denied US use of military bases at Rota and Morón. PM Sánchez declared US-Israel war a "big error" dragging Eastern Mediterranean into instability. Trump threatened embargo in response.

🇳🇴 Norway

FM Espen Barth Eide: "The attack is described by Israel as a preventive strike, but it is not in line with international law. Preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat."

🇨🇳 China

FM Wang Yi told Iran "talking is always better than fighting" and urged seizing "every opportunity and window for peace." Criticized US-Israeli strikes as sovereignty violation but also concerned about Iranian strikes on Gulf states where China has commercial interests.

🇷🇺 Russia

FM Lavrov urged Iran to end hostilities while condemning US-Israeli strikes near Bushehr nuclear plant as "extremely dangerous."

🇶🇦 Qatar

FM spokesperson: "diplomacy should be the path to ending the conflict." Arrested members of Iranian sleeper cell in early March. Seven killed in helicopter crash March 22.

🇵🇰 Pakistan

PM Sharif offered to host negotiations between US and Iran. Protesters attempted to storm US Consulate in Karachi.

🇱🇧 Lebanon

Declared Iranian ambassador "persona non grata" and recalled envoy from Tehran. Over 1,070 killed and 700,000 displaced since conflict escalated. Warned of potential Israeli ground invasion.

🇦🇷 Argentina

President Milei: "proud to be the most Zionist president in the world," called Iran "our enemy" citing past bombings.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Peace talks: Conflicting claims persist. Trump claims "productive conversations" occurred March 23, but Iran's Foreign Ministry denies any negotiations. An Iranian source told CNN there has been "outreach" and Tehran willing to listen to "suitable" proposals. White House says situation is "fluid."

War duration: Trump initially projected 4-5 weeks. Analysts warn Iran is "a vast country" and regime may prove more resilient. Carnegie analyst notes Iran has "10 out of 10 resolve" as regime survival is existential. Former UK ambassador believes Iran cannot sustain retaliation "indefinitely."

Israel's independent track: Israel has stated it is not part of US-Iran talks and will continue operations until Iran's capabilities eliminated. Israeli ground invasion of Lebanon possible.

Economic trajectory: Unclear if Strait of Hormuz can be fully reopened. IEA chief warns crisis is "very severe." Iran's economy already devastated; new regime inherits "wrecked economy."

Regime stability: RAND experts say Islamic Republic "will be fundamentally altered" even if it survives. New Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei's selection was controversial and contravened his father's written wishes.
