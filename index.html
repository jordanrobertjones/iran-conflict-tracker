📋 What Happened — Background

March 03, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated military strikes on Iran—codenamed Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The strikes targeted key Iranian officials, military commanders, facilities, and aimed at regime change. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in the opening strikes on Leadership House, along with senior officials including Ali Shamkhani.

The joint US-Israeli operation has struck over 2,500 targets across 24 of Iran's 31 provinces, including Tehran, Isfahan, Qom, Karaj, and Kermanshah. Key targets included the IRGC Malek-Ashtar building, the Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting (IRIB) headquarters, Iran's parliament building, and the Assembly of Experts during a meeting to elect a new supreme leader. CENTCOM describes the opening 24 hours as "double the scale" of the 2003 Iraq War.

Iran has responded with Operation True Promise IV, launching retaliatory missile and drone attacks on Israel, US military bases across nine countries (Bahrain, Iraq, Jordan, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Cyprus), and civilian targets in Gulf states. The IRGC has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, threatening to "set ablaze" any vessel attempting passage. Qatar has shut down LNG production at Ras Laffan after Iranian drone strikes.

Day 4 (March 3) developments: US death toll rises to six service members with 18 wounded. Three US F-15 jets crashed in Kuwait due to friendly fire. Iranian Red Crescent reports over 787 civilian deaths in Iran, including at least 165 schoolgirls killed in a strike on a girls' elementary school in Minab. Hezbollah has entered the conflict, launching missiles at Israel "in revenge for Khamenei's death," prompting Israeli strikes on Lebanon killing at least 50. Israel reports 11 civilian deaths; UAE reports 3 foreign worker deaths.

✅ Arguments For the War

Eliminates nuclear threat timeline: Netanyahu argues Iran's underground bunkers would have become "immune within months." Foreign Minister Gideon Sa'ar stated delay "would have allowed the Iranian regime to reach a level of immunity for its nuclear programme."
Decapitation of hostile regime: The killing of Khamenei and senior IRGC commanders removes leadership that openly called for Israel's destruction for decades. The IDF destroyed the Supreme National Security Council headquarters and IRGC command infrastructure.
Military degradation achieved: Trump claims Iran has "no navy, air force, air detection, or radar" remaining. The combined force has achieved air superiority over Tehran and struck over 600 terror infrastructures.
Gulf states aligning against Iran: According to Reuters analysis, Iran's attacks on Gulf states have caused them to realize Iran poses a direct threat. UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, and Bahrain have condemned Iranian attacks as "a blatant violation of sovereignty." Lebanon has announced it will ban Hezbollah's military activities.
Iranian diaspora support: Celebratory rallies were held worldwide by the Iranian diaspora following the strikes, with Iranians waving anti-Islamic Republic symbols including the Lion and Sun flag.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Massive civilian casualties: Over 787 Iranian civilians reported killed, including at least 165 schoolgirls at a girls' elementary school in Minab. The Washington Post and New York Times verified footage of the school attack. UNESCO called it "a grave violation of humanitarian law."
Regional war expanding: The conflict has spread to Lebanon (Hezbollah involvement), nine Arab states under Iranian attack, and Cyprus (UK RAF base struck). The Washington Post reports the conflict now threatens 300 million civilians across more than a dozen nations.
Strait of Hormuz closure: Iran has effectively closed the critical waterway, with ship traffic dropping to near zero. Oil prices rose ~10%, with analysts forecasting $100/barrel if disruption persists. Qatar has halted LNG production. European natural gas futures jumped 30%.
No congressional authorization: Stimson Center experts call the war "unconstitutional, unwise" noting "just 5 percent of Democrats, and only 17 percent of Republicans" support a regime-change war. Critics cite violation of War Powers requirements.
Regime change unlikely via airpower: CNN analysis notes "it's hard to think of a single example of air power triggering regime change and the birth of a stable successor state." The regime has survived prior protests through mass violence.
Diplomatic process destroyed: Oman's Foreign Minister said just before strikes that a "breakthrough" had been reached with Iran agreeing to never stockpile enriched uranium. IAEA Director General stated the agency has "no indication" of nuclear weapons installations being hit and has seen no "structured program" to manufacture weapons.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Will the regime fall? CNN analysis notes the chances of regime collapse "in a repressive state that penetrates every level of Iranian society seem far-fetched." Khamenei's martyrdom may make loyalists "even more ruthless." An Interim Leadership Council has been announced; the 88-member Assembly of Experts will choose a successor—contenders include hardliners, moderates, and Khamenei's son.

How long will this last? Trump says 4-5 weeks but could go "far longer." Israel believes it can achieve goals in two weeks. Gen. Dan Caine warned this is "not a single, overnight operation" and more US losses should be expected.

Will the Strait of Hormuz remain closed? Analysts say a "total shutdown" long-term is "completely unlikely" as Iran would be "tightening the noose around its own neck." However, with no P&I insurance available from March 5 and at least 150 ships stranded, the strait is effectively closed for commercial traffic. Trump says US Navy will escort tankers "if necessary."

Will Russia/China intervene? Analysts conclude neither can provide meaningful military support—Russia is "overstretched" in Ukraine, and China prioritizes "preserving détente with the US." Chatham House notes Russia "cannot militarily balance the US-Israel coalition" and "lacks the economic weight to fully underwrite Tehran."

What happens if regime survives? Russian FM Lavrov warns the war could paradoxically cause Iran to pursue nuclear weapons: "The US doesn't attack those who have nuclear bombs." Arab states may also join a nuclear race.
