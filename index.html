📋 What Happened — Background

March 08, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States (Operation Epic Fury) and Israel (Operation Roaring Lion) launched coordinated military strikes against Iran, targeting leadership, military installations, missile production sites, and the remnants of Iran's nuclear program. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in the initial strikes, along with dozens of senior military figures. The operation followed failed negotiations over Iran's nuclear program and was preceded by massive anti-regime protests in Iran in early 2026 that were brutally suppressed.

As of March 8, 2026 (Day 9), U.S. Central Command reports over 3,000 targets struck in Iran and 43 Iranian warships destroyed. Iran has retaliated by launching over 500 ballistic missiles and 2,000 drones at Israel and U.S. bases across 9 countries in the Gulf region. At least 1,332 people have been killed in Iran, 11 in Israel, and 6 U.S. soldiers in Kuwait. The conflict has now expanded to include Israeli strikes on Tehran oil depots and a renewed Israeli ground operation in Lebanon following Hezbollah rocket attacks.

President Trump is demanding Iran's "unconditional surrender" and has warned that Iran "will be hit very hard." Iran's Foreign Minister has rejected ceasefire negotiations, and Iran's interim leadership council—formed after Khamenei's death—has vowed retaliation. The White House has indicated the campaign could last 4-6 weeks, with estimated costs of $891 million per day.

✅ Arguments For the War

Elimination of Iranian Nuclear Threat: U.S. officials state the operation aims to permanently prevent Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons, with significant damage to missile production facilities.
Degradation of Iran's Military Capability: According to CENTCOM, Iran's ballistic missile retaliation has decreased 90%, its navy is "wiped out" with 43 ships destroyed, and Israel claims 80% of Iran's air defenses and 60% of its missile launch capability have been eliminated.
Weakened Proxy Networks: The operation targets Iran's "axis of resistance," including strikes on Hezbollah commanders in Beirut. Analysts note Iran's regional allies were already significantly weakened by prior Israeli military actions.
Regime Destabilization: The killing of Khamenei and dozens of senior officials has created "strategic vertigo" in Iran's leadership. Reports indicate many Iranian officers have abandoned their positions, and the military has lost control over several units.
Support from Some Regional Actors: Saudi Arabia's Defense Minister reportedly warned about risks of not attacking Iran. Finland's Foreign Minister said Khamenei's assassination "opens a window for long-awaited change." NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte said Europe is "supportive" and that the U.S. "knows what it is doing."

❌ Arguments Against the War

Humanitarian Costs: At least 1,332 dead in Iran including civilian casualties; WHO reports 13 Iranian health facilities hit; strikes damaged schools, hospitals, the Grand Bazaar, and Golestan Palace (UNESCO site). The UN declared a "major humanitarian emergency" affecting 25 million people as refugees.
Regional Escalation: Iran has struck 9 Gulf countries; a Bahrain desalination plant was damaged; Dubai International Airport was hit; a UK base in Cyprus was struck; Azerbaijan is now preparing retaliatory measures after Iranian drone strikes.
International Law Concerns: France's Macron said strikes were "outside of international law"; Norway's Foreign Minister said the attack is "not in line with international law"; Human Rights Watch called for war crimes investigations; Spain declared the war a "big error."
Uncertain Endgame: Experts at Brookings and CSIS warn the operation may become "more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers anticipated." War aims have shifted multiple times. Only 25% of Americans support the attacks according to Reuters/IPSOS polling.
Economic Disruption: Oil prices have risen ~20% to $80/barrel; estimated costs are $891M/day (~$3.7B in first 100 hours); Gulf economies face potential "catastrophe"; shipping has stopped through Strait of Hormuz; stock markets have fallen.
Strategic Risks: A Foreign Policy survey of 949 IR scholars found most believe the war will harm U.S. security, increase terrorist attack risks, and increase the likelihood China attacks Taiwan. Russia is reportedly providing Iran with intelligence on U.S. positions.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

United States: President Trump demands "unconditional surrender"; claims Operation Epic Fury is "meeting or surpassing all of its goals"; Senate blocked war powers resolution 47-53.
Israel: PM Netanyahu says alliance with U.S. is "deeper than ever"; IDF claims 80% of Iran's air defenses destroyed; Israel launched ground invasion of Lebanon on March 3.
UK: PM Starmer approved use of British bases for "defensive US action" after initial hesitation; HMS Dragon deployed to Eastern Mediterranean.
Finland: FM Valtonen welcomed Khamenei's death as opening "window for long-awaited change."
NATO: Secretary General Rutte said Europe is "supportive" of U.S. attacks on Iran.
France: President Macron said strikes are conducted "outside of international law" which France "cannot approve of."
Spain: PM Sánchez declared "No to war," blocked use of bases, called war a "big error"; Trump threatened trade embargo.
Russia: FM Lavrov called strikes "a deliberate, premeditated, and unprovoked act of armed aggression"; Putin reportedly offering Iran intelligence.
China: Foreign Ministry called Khamenei's killing "a grave violation of Iran's sovereignty"; PLA released video captioned "Prepare For War At Any Time."
Norway: FM Eide said attack is "not in line with international law."
Turkey: Maintaining "non-aligned" stance; FM Fidan leading diplomatic push for ceasefire; intercepted Iranian missile headed toward Turkish airspace.
Gulf Cooperation Council: Emergency meeting demanded Iran cease attacks on Arab countries; Qatar shot down two Iranian Su-24 bombers.
Iran: President Pezeshkian apologized to Gulf neighbors for strikes but vowed to never surrender; FM rejected ceasefire negotiations.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Duration and Scope: The White House suggests 4-6 weeks, but experts warn the conflict could become protracted. Trump has signaled expanding targets to "new areas and groups of people."

Regime Change Feasibility: Analysts debate whether air power alone can achieve regime change without ground forces, which the U.S. has expressed reluctance to deploy.

Iranian Resilience: ACLED notes "the Islamic Republic is more likely to continue fighting than to concede." Iran's interim leadership has vowed continued resistance.

Strait of Hormuz: Iran has threatened to target any U.S. or Israeli ships attempting passage. Traffic has effectively stopped, but the strait technically remains open.

Third-Party Escalation: Azerbaijan is preparing retaliatory measures after being struck; Turkey intercepted an Iranian missile; potential for conflict to draw in additional actors remains high.
