📋 What Happened — Background

March 04, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated military strikes against Iran in operations codenamed Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The strikes targeted Iran's leadership, military commanders, nuclear program, missile sites, and air defenses with the stated aim of regime change and preventing Iran from obtaining nuclear weapons. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in the initial strikes, along with dozens of other Iranian officials.

As of March 4, 2026 (Day 5), the conflict has entered its most intense phase. Over 1,145 people have been killed in Iran according to state media, including a disputed strike on a girls' elementary school in Minab that killed over 160 students. Six US service members have been killed and 18 wounded. Iran has retaliated with Operation True Promise IV, launching over 500 ballistic missiles and 2,000+ drones at Israel, US military bases across 9 countries, and Gulf state infrastructure. The conflict has spread to Lebanon, with Hezbollah joining on March 2. Iranian naval frigates IRIS Jamaran and IRIS Dena have been sunk. The IRGC reports the US-Israel coalition has struck over 2,500 targets across 24 of Iran's 31 provinces.

Iran has established an Interim Leadership Council under Ali Larijani, and funeral ceremonies for Khamenei are underway. Trump has predicted the war could last 4-5 weeks, while CENTCOM reports Iran's air defenses and missile capabilities are "severely degraded." Major airports across the Gulf remain closed, with airspace restrictions affecting millions of travelers. Oil prices have surged and stock markets have experienced significant declines.

✅ Arguments For the War

Eliminating nuclear threat: CSIS analysts assess strikes may have "significantly reduced the risk of short-term Iranian proliferation" by targeting nuclear facilities. Iran still possesses 400kg of 60% enriched uranium at unknown locations.
Military degradation: CENTCOM reports Iran's "air defenses and hundreds of ballistic missile launchers and drones" are "severely degraded." 17 Iranian ships destroyed including most operational submarine. Air superiority achieved over Tehran.
Regime weakness: Iran's regime was at its weakest point due to extensive protests in early 2026, economic collapse (70%+ food inflation), and weakened regional proxies. Some see opportunity for democratic transition.
Regional realignment: Gulf states (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain) have condemned Iranian retaliatory strikes as "violations of sovereignty," potentially strengthening US alliance structure.
Support from allies: Ukraine, Australia, Canada, Czech Republic, Finland, Latvia, Lithuania, and others have expressed support for the US-Israel operation.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Civilian casualties: Over 1,145 Iranians killed including disputed strike on girls' school killing 160+ children. Human Rights Activists News Agency reports rising death toll. UN Secretary-General condemned both sides.
Regional escalation: Conflict has spread to 12+ countries. Iran attacked US bases in Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Jordan, Iraq. UK base in Cyprus struck. Hezbollah entered war. 40+ killed in Lebanon.
Legal/constitutional concerns: No UN authorization, no Congressional declaration of war. Democrats call it "unwarranted and illegal." War Powers Resolution challenges expected.
Domestic opposition: CNN poll shows 60% of Americans disapprove. Protests across US cities. Democratic military veterans are "most vocal critics."
Economic fallout: Dow fell 1,110+ points. Oil prices surging. 150 ships stalled at Strait of Hormuz. Gulf airports closed. Analysts warn of "catastrophe" for Gulf economies.
Regime change skepticism: Experts warn "You cannot facilitate regime change through air strikes alone" (Michael Mulroy). Iran's security apparatus may survive and become more radical. Khamenei's death may "make his street-level loyalists even more ruthless."
Nuclear proliferation risks: CSIS warns if Iran's Atomic Energy Organization collapses, "Iranian nuclear scientists could pose proliferation risks to non-state actors."

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Can airstrikes alone achieve regime change? Military analysts like former Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense Michael Mulroy warn: "You cannot facilitate regime change through air strikes alone. If anyone is left alive to speak, the regime is still there."

Will the Iranian regime collapse or consolidate? The Interim Leadership Council has formed under Ali Larijani. Analysts warn Khamenei's "martyrdom may make his street-level loyalists even more ruthless." Some Iranians rallying to government after foreign attacks.

Will the conflict escalate further? Senator Blumenthal says he is "more fearful than ever" of US ground troops. Iran's IRGC announced "ground forces entered battlefield operations." Turkish interception of Iranian missile suggests further geographic expansion.

What happens to Iran's nuclear scientists and material? Iran still possesses 400kg of 60% enriched uranium at unknown locations. CSIS warns scattered nuclear scientists "could pose proliferation risks to non-state actors."

How will Gulf states respond? Iranian attacks on their infrastructure may push them closer to US alliance, but could also destabilize the region's economic infrastructure.
