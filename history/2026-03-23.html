📋 What Happened — Background

March 23, 2026

The US-Israel military campaign against Iran, known as Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel), began on February 28, 2026. The conflict is now in its 24th day, with escalating tensions over the Strait of Hormuz. Over 1,500 civilians have been killed in Iran, at least 18 in Israel, 13 US service members, and more than 1,000 in Lebanon from the parallel conflict with Hezbollah.

On March 22-23, Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum threatening to "hit and obliterate" Iran's power plants if Tehran does not fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz. The deadline expires at 23:44 GMT on March 23. Iran has responded by threatening to permanently close the strait and mine the Persian Gulf waters if its coastline or islands are attacked. Israel launched "extensive" strikes on Tehran on March 23.

The International Energy Agency's head Fatih Birol warned the crisis is "very severe" and worse than the two oil crises of the 1970s combined. Oil prices have reached $114/barrel (Brent) and $100/barrel (WTI). Over 3,000 vessels remain stranded in the region due to near-total halt of Hormuz traffic. Trump has suggested he is considering "winding down" military efforts while ruling out a ceasefire.

✅ Arguments For the War

✓
Degradation of Iranian military capabilities
US forces have struck over 8,000 targets, destroyed 30+ Iranian warships, reduced missile/drone attacks by 90-95%, and established air dominance over Iranian airspace within 24 hours.
✓
Nuclear program disruption
CSIS and other analysts note strikes have targeted remnants of Iran's nuclear infrastructure, following prior damage from June 2025 strikes, further delaying any weapons capability.
✓
Proxy network weakened
Iran's "forward defense" doctrine and Axis of Resistance have been significantly degraded; Hezbollah, Hamas, and Iraqi militias fighting for organizational survival rather than advancing Tehran's objectives.
✓
Regional security coalition forming
22 nations have expressed willingness to help secure the Strait of Hormuz. Washington Institute analysts argue Iran's attacks on Gulf states have underscored that threats "are better countered collectively than individually."

❌ Arguments Against the War

✗
Global economic crisis
IEA chief warns of "major, major threat" to global economy worse than 1970s oil crises. Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, stranding 3,000+ vessels. Goldman Sachs projects elevated oil prices through 2027.
✗
Diplomatic breakthrough abandoned
Oman's FM said peace was "within reach" on Feb 27 with Iran agreeing to IAEA verification and uranium downgrade. RAND/Brookings experts describe this as a "war of choice" that morphed into "war of necessity."
✗
Civilian casualties mounting
1,500+ killed in Iran including 204+ children per reports. WHO identified 13+ health infrastructure sites hit. UN experts characterized some strikes as potential war crimes under Rome Statute. UNESCO sites damaged.
✗
No clear endgame or exit strategy
Carnegie expert Sadjadpour: "I don't think President Trump understood what he was getting into." ACLED warns war "may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated."
✗
Regional escalation risk
Iran has struck 9+ countries. Missiles fired at Diego Garcia (3,800km away). Threats to attack Gulf desalination plants and energy infrastructure. Lebanon death toll over 1,000 as Israel prepares ground invasion.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Power plant strikes: Trump's 48-hour ultimatum expires late March 23. Iran has threatened permanent Hormuz closure and attacks on regional infrastructure if its power grid is targeted—potentially catastrophic escalation.

War duration: Trump signals "winding down" but unclear timeline. Iran rejects ceasefire. ACLED/RAND warn conflict likely more protracted than anticipated as regime "more likely to continue fighting than to concede."

Strait of Hormuz: Iran says strait open to non-"enemies" but insurers refusing coverage. Plans to monetize waterway access. 3,000+ vessels stranded. Potential US/allied naval escort operations being discussed.

Ground operations: Axios reports US considering blockade/occupation of Kharg Island. Israel preparing expanded Lebanon ground invasion. Some analysts suggest possible coastal SOF operations in Iran to secure Strait.
