📋 What Happened — Background

March 07, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated military operations against Iran—dubbed Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The strikes began at approximately 7:00 AM local time and targeted Iranian leadership, military installations, missile production sites, and remnants of Iran's nuclear program. Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed in the opening salvo, along with dozens of senior officials.

As of March 7, 2026 (Day 8), the conflict has entered its second week with no signs of slowing. The US military has struck more than 3,000 targets in Iran and destroyed 43 Iranian warships. At least 1,332 people have been killed in Iran, including at least 181 children according to UNICEF. Six US servicemembers and 11 Israelis have also been killed. Over 200 people have been killed in Lebanon since Hezbollah joined the conflict on March 2.

The White House has suggested the campaign may last 4-6 weeks. Trump is demanding Iran's "unconditional surrender" before any negotiations. Iran has rejected ceasefire talks, with President Pezeshkian stating Iran will not capitulate. Russia is reportedly providing Iran with intelligence on US military positions.

The conflict has spread across the region: Iran has launched over 500 ballistic missiles and 2,000 drones at Israel and US bases in Kuwait, Qatar, UAE, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and Jordan. Israel has conducted 2,500 strikes and claims 80% of Iran's air defenses have been destroyed. A stray Iranian missile struck Turkey's Hatay Province, prompting NATO discussions.

✅ Arguments For the War

✓
Elimination of hostile leadership: Supreme Leader Khamenei, multiple IRGC commanders, and dozens of senior officials were killed, potentially creating opportunity for political change in Iran.
✓
Degradation of Iran's military capabilities: US claims Iran's ballistic missile attacks have fallen 90% and drone attacks 83% since the war began. Iran's navy has been largely destroyed with 43 warships sunk.
✓
Weakening Iran-Russia military ties: With Iran forced to divert resources to its own defense, its support to Russia in Ukraine (drones, military equipment) could be reduced.
✓
Near-complete air superiority: Israel claims to have established "near-complete air superiority" and destroyed 80% of Iran's air defense systems, limiting Iran's ability to resist further operations.

❌ Arguments Against the War

✗
Significant civilian casualties: At least 1,332 killed in Iran including 181 children. CNN evidence suggests US was responsible for a strike on an elementary school that killed scores of children. WHO reports 13 healthcare sites hit.
✗
Regional destabilization: Conflict has spread to Lebanon (95,000+ displaced, 123+ killed), Iraq, and Gulf states. Iran striking US allies including Kuwait, Qatar, UAE, Bahrain, and Saudi Arabia. Hezbollah has rejoined conflict.
✗
Massive economic costs: CSIS estimates Operation Epic Fury costs ~$891 million/day, with $3.5 billion already unbudgeted. Oil prices surged 31% (US) and 24% (Brent). Dow Jones fell 1,000+ points. Global shipping disrupted.
✗
Unclear endgame and risk of prolonged conflict: Experts warn the war could become protracted. Trump's regime change goals may require ground troops, which US has been reluctant to deploy. Only 25% of Americans support the war.
✗
International law concerns: Multiple European leaders (Macron, Sanchez, Italy's defense minister) have stated the strikes violate international law. Iran's UN ambassador accuses US-Israel of war crimes.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States: Senate (47-53) and House (212-219) blocked war powers resolutions. Trump demanding "unconditional surrender," claims Iran being "demolished." White House projects 4-6 week campaign.
🇮🇱 Israel: Conducted 2,500 strikes, called up 70,000 reservists. Claims "near-complete air superiority." Authorized ground invasion of Lebanon. Moving to "next phase" of war.
🇩🇪 Germany: Chancellor Merz called Iranian regime "a terrorist regime" and said Germany shares US-Israel goals. Declined to "lecture" US on international law.
🇬🇧 United Kingdom: Initially refused base access, but now permitting "defensive US action" from British bases including RAF Fairford. Sent HMS Dragon and helicopters to Cyprus.
🇫🇷 France: Macron said strikes "conducted outside of international law" but French bases now supporting US operations. Sent warship to Cyprus.
🇪🇸 Spain: Called war an "extraordinary mistake" and "illegal war devoid of an effective strategy." Maintaining consistent position on international law.
🇮🇹 Italy: Defense minister said US-Israel attack violated international law, even while sending defensive naval assets to Cyprus and Gulf allies.
🇷🇺 Russia: Putin spoke with Pezeshkian offering condolences. Reportedly providing Iran intelligence on US military positions. Benefiting from increased oil demand.
🇪🇺 European Union: Called for "maximum restraint" and respect for international law. Condemned Iranian retaliatory strikes on Gulf states as "unjustifiable." Von der Leyen initially backed "credible transition" (regime change).
🇹🇷 Turkey: Adopting "non-aligned" stance. Foreign Minister Fidan leading diplomatic push for ceasefire. Iran struck Hatay Province (attributed to "technical anomaly").
🇶🇦 Qatar: Condemned Iranian strikes as violation of sovereignty, summoned Iran's ambassador, but urged immediate return to dialogue. Suspended all flights.
🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia: Signaled readiness to take "any measures" for protection including retaliation, while GCC called for diplomatic coordination.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Duration and scope: White House suggests 4-6 weeks, but experts warn it could become protracted. Pentagon has declined to give timeline. Regime change goals may require ground troops the US is reluctant to deploy.

Iranian succession: Election for new Supreme Leader pending. CIA assesses hardliner from IRGC likely to replace Khamenei. Trump has said Mojtaba Khamenei (son) is "unacceptable" and wants to be "personally involved" in selecting Iran's next leader.

Strait of Hormuz: Iran has stated it will target any US or Israeli ships passing through. The strait remains technically open but shipping giants Maersk and others have suspended operations. Oil storage filling up across region.

European involvement: UK, France now providing base access for operations. Iran has warned European countries that join will become "legitimate targets." Risk of "mission creep" drawing Europe deeper into conflict.

Houthi response: Yemen's Houthis have condemned attacks on Iran but have not resumed Red Sea shipping attacks as they did in support of Hamas—a key uncertainty for global trade.
