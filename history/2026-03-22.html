📋 What Happened — Background

March 22, 2026

Operation Epic Fury, launched on February 28, 2026, is a joint US-Israeli military campaign against Iran that began with nearly 900 strikes in the first 12 hours. The opening strikes killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior Iranian officials, with his son Mojtaba Khamenei elected as successor on March 8. The operation has severely degraded Iran's military capabilities: according to the U.S. Department of Defense, Iranian missile and drone attacks are down 90% from the early days of the war. Over 70% of Iran's missile launchers have been neutralized and missile production reduced to zero, according to Israeli intelligence estimates.

As the conflict enters its fourth week (Day 23-24), key developments include: Iranian missile strikes on Dimona and Arad on March 21-22 injured over 180 people after Israeli air defenses failed to intercept the missiles, marking the first successful strike near Israel's nuclear research facility. President Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum threatening to "obliterate" Iranian power plants if the Strait of Hormuz is not reopened. Iran responded by threatening to target all U.S. energy infrastructure in the region if attacked. The humanitarian toll continues to rise, with over 1,330 civilians killed in Iran (including at least 200 children) and more than 18,000 injured, according to the Iranian Red Crescent.

✅ Arguments For the War

⚔️Iranian military capability severely degraded: missile launches down 90%, drone launches down 83%, and 70%+ of missile launchers destroyed, according to U.S. Central Command
☢️Nuclear infrastructure damaged: Natanz and Fordow facilities struck, defense industrial base systematically targeted, setting back weapons programs by years per Israeli intelligence
🎯Decapitation of Iranian leadership achieved: Supreme Leader Khamenei and dozens of senior officials killed in opening strikes, disrupting command structure
✈️Complete air superiority established: U.S. flying non-stealth B-1 bombers over Iranian airspace, with no confirmed loss of American or Israeli aircraft
🚢Iranian naval threat eliminated: 19 Iranian warships and 1 submarine destroyed, naval infrastructure repeatedly targeted per CENTCOM

❌ Arguments Against the War

⛽Global energy crisis: Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, oil prices surged 45% since war began to over $110/barrel, over 3,000 vessels stranded in the Middle East
🩸Heavy civilian casualties: Over 1,330 Iranians killed (including 200+ children), 18,000+ injured; 180+ Israelis injured in Dimona/Arad strikes; more than 1,000 killed in Lebanon since March 2
🌍Regional destabilization: Gulf states under sustained Iranian attack—Bahrain intercepted 143 missiles and 242 drones; attacks on Kuwait refineries, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iraq, Qatar, and Cyprus
⚖️Legal concerns: Norway's Foreign Minister said strikes "not in line with international law"; UN human rights experts characterized strike on girls' school as potential war crime; war launched without Congressional approval
🎯Defense failures exposed: Israeli air defenses failed to intercept missiles hitting Dimona and Arad near nuclear facility; Iran claims this signals "new phase of battle"
🔄Diplomacy undermined: Omani-mediated talks showed "real progress" days before strikes; Foreign Minister Al-Busaidi said "active and serious negotiations" were undermined

Trump: "Their leadership is gone, their navy and air force are dead... I don't want a deal." Issued 48-hour ultimatum to reopen Hormuz or face strikes on power plants. VP Vance reportedly expressed private skepticism.

🇮🇱 IsraelLeading Operation

PM Netanyahu pledged to fight on "all fronts"; Defense Minister said strikes will "increase significantly" this week. IDF Chief Zamir said campaign at "halfway" stage.

🇬🇧 United KingdomLimited Support

PM Starmer: "I do not believe in regime change from the skies." UK not participating in strikes but allowing U.S. bombers to use British bases including Diego Garcia for "defensive" operations.

🇪🇺 European UnionCautious

🇳🇴 NorwayCritical

Foreign Minister Eide: "The attack is described by Israel as a preventive strike, but it is not in line with international law. Preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat."

🇸🇦 Saudi ArabiaUnder Attack

🇧🇭 BahrainSupportive

First regional country to signal willingness to help reopen Strait of Hormuz. Has intercepted 143 missiles and 242 drones since February 28.

NATODivided

Secretary General Rutte said Europe is "supportive" as Iran is a "threat." However, Australia, Germany, Greece, Italy, Japan, and UK all declined Trump's request to send warships to secure Hormuz.

🇶🇦 QatarCritical

🇨🇳 ChinaOpposed

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Strait of Hormuz Resolution: Trump's 48-hour ultimatum expires ~Monday evening. Iran threatened to target all U.S. energy infrastructure in the region if power plants are struck. With 22 nations pledging support to reopen the strait, the next 48 hours are critical.

Escalation to Civilian Infrastructure: Trump's threat to target power plants would expand strikes to infrastructure affecting daily civilian life—a significant escalation. Iran's response threatens regional energy, IT, and desalination infrastructure.

Ground Forces Question: Despite Trump's denials, thousands more Marines and sailors are deploying to the region. Analysts suggest physically securing Hormuz may require ground presence—a scenario the administration hasn't ruled out.

Nuclear Dimension: Reports suggest the Trump administration is strategizing methods to secure or extract Iran's nuclear materials. The timing remains unclear. Iran successfully struck near Israel's Dimona nuclear facility, signaling capability to target nuclear sites.

Duration Uncertain: Trump said campaign could "wind down" but Israel says strikes will "increase significantly" this week. ACLED analysts warn Iran is "more likely to continue fighting than to concede."
