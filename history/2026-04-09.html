📋 What Happened — Background

April 09, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched a massive coordinated military campaign against Iran — codenamed Operation Epic Fury (U.S.) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel) — the most significant U.S. military action in the Middle East since the Iraq War. On February 27 at 3:38 p.m. EST, Donald Trump, traveling on Air Force One to Corpus Christi, Texas, gave the order to proceed with Operation Epic Fury. In a coordinated strike, over 100 aircraft from sea and land struck over 1,000 Iranian targets within 24 hours, including command centers, missile infrastructure, and naval assets. The stated objectives were to destroy Iran's ballistic missile infrastructure and nuclear program, eliminate IRGC leadership, annihilate Iran's naval capabilities, and neutralize Iranian-backed regional proxies.

Just before the strikes began, on February 27, Oman's Foreign Minister Badr Al-Busaidi said a "breakthrough" had been reached and Iran had agreed both to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full verification by the IAEA. Al-Busaidi said peace was "within reach." After the U.S. and Israel attacked Iran, Al-Busaidi said that he was dismayed and that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined. Iran's Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and a number of Iranian officials, including the Minister of Defense Aziz Nasirzadeh and the head of Iran's Revolutionary Guard Mohammad Pakpour, were killed in the strikes.

Iran responded with missile and drone strikes against Israel, U.S. bases, and U.S.-allied countries in the Middle East, and by closing the Strait of Hormuz, disrupting global trade. The war's economic impact, described as the world's largest oil supply disruption since the 1970s energy crisis, includes surges in oil and gas prices, wide disruptions in aviation and tourism, and volatility in financial markets.

🕊️ April 8, 2026 — Fragile Ceasefire: The U.S. and Iran agreed to a two-week truce on April 7, just an hour before Trump's deadline to escalate the conflict expired. Iran accepted the ceasefire, with talks set to begin in Pakistan's capital, Islamabad, after the U.S. agreed to suspend attacks on the condition that Tehran fully reopens the Strait of Hormuz. The truce, brokered by Pakistan, follows fierce exchanges of air strikes, missile attacks and threats that saw unprecedented strikes on Gulf nations, disrupted global shipping routes and heightened fears of a prolonged confrontation. The White House announced the U.S. would hold direct talks with Iran, with Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt saying Vice President JD Vance will lead a delegation to Islamabad, which would include special envoy Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner.

⚠️ Day 41 — Ceasefire Under Immediate Strain (April 9, 2026): Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu rejected Pakistan's inclusion of Lebanon in the truce, asserting the ceasefire "does not include Lebanon," and Israel launched what it described as the strongest wave of attacks on Lebanon since the start of the war. Iran's parliamentary speaker Ghalibaf said three parts of Iran's 10-point ceasefire proposal had been violated: Israel's continued attacks on Lebanon, the entry of a drone into Iranian airspace, and the denial of Iran's right to enrich uranium. "In such a situation, a bilateral ceasefire or negotiations is unreasonable," he said. Iran has informed mediators that it will only participate in talks in Pakistan if the ceasefire includes Lebanon, and has threatened to reverse its decision to reopen the Strait of Hormuz if IDF strikes on Hezbollah continue. The first round of talks is set for Saturday morning local time, even as the Strait of Hormuz remained largely closed as of Thursday morning.

✅ Arguments For the War

🎯
Massive Degradation of Iran's Military-Industrial Base
General Dan Caine reported that around 90% of Iran's weapons factories, more than 80% of its missile facilities, and nearly 80% of its nuclear industrial base had been hit. "Every factory that produced Shahed one-way attack drones was struck," Caine said. U.S. forces hit more than 13,000 targets during the war, destroying 80% of Iran's air defenses as well as "more than 450 ballistic missile storage facilities" and "800 one-way attack drone storage facilities."
☢️
Nuclear Program Severely Set Back
Operation Midnight Hammer in June 2025 dealt a significant blow to Iran's nuclear program, and the 2026 strikes further targeted remaining capabilities. The 2025 strikes decimated Iran's enrichment facilities at Fordow and Natanz and destroyed Iran's metallurgy facilities at Isfahan. U.S. officials now insist on zero enrichment as a condition of any final deal.
🛡️
Naval Threat Neutralized
U.S. forces confirmed Iran's naval capability in the Gulf of Oman has been neutralized, reporting the sinking of all 11 previously active Iranian warships. Secretary Hegseth revealed Iran was "obsessed" with trying to strike a U.S. aircraft carrier during the operation but "never got even close," firing hundreds of missiles and drones that were "easily shot down miles and miles away."
🕊️
Ceasefire Opens Diplomatic Window
White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt described the ceasefire as a calculated move, arguing it "created maximum leverage" for Trump to pursue tough negotiations, opening the door to a diplomatic solution and long-term peace. Trump said Washington would "work closely with Iran," which "has gone through what will be a very productive regime change."
💬
Bipartisan & Allied Support for Degrading Iran
Republican Sen. Mitch McConnell voiced support for the war, saying "I support what the president is trying to do" and noting Iran "has been at war with us for 47 years." McConnell added: "No matter how this ends, they've been significantly diminished. Their ability to hurt others, significantly diminished."

❌ Arguments Against the War

💀
Significant U.S. and Civilian Casualties
Thirteen U.S. service members have died during Operation Epic Fury. Iranian authorities say that more than 2,000 people have been killed in U.S.-Israeli strikes since the start of the war, with a large number of residential units, schools, hospitals, and historical sites impacted. The conflict between Hezbollah and Israel escalated into the 2026 Lebanon war, killing more than 1,400 people, both militants and civilians, by early April.
🌊
Catastrophic Global Energy Disruption
The effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz has caused the biggest oil supply shock on record, choking off roughly 12–15 million barrels of crude oil per day. The war has caused Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, the UAE, Qatar and Bahrain to collectively shut in an estimated 7.5 million barrels per day in March, with this historic supply disruption expected to rise to 9.1 million barrels per day in April. The EIA now expects gas prices to peak at $4.30 a gallon.
☢️
Nuclear Damage Assessment Disputed — Enrichment May Resume
A new U.S. assessment found that only one of three Iranian nuclear enrichment sites struck was mostly destroyed, while the two others were not as badly damaged and may have been degraded only to a point where nuclear enrichment could resume within months if Iran wants it to. The IAEA said while Iran has an "ambitious" nuclear program and has refused inspections of damaged sites, there was no evidence of a structured nuclear weapons program when the 2026 war began.
⚖️
Diplomatic Momentum Destroyed — Talks Were Progressing
Just before the strikes began, Oman's FM said a "breakthrough" had been reached and Iran had agreed both to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full verification by the IAEA. Critics, including legal and international relations experts, have described the attacks as illegal under U.S. law and a violation of Iran's sovereignty under international law.
🚨
Ceasefire Fragile — Lebanon Dispute Threatens Collapse
CSIS analysts warn the ceasefire masks a far more dangerous reality: Iran's nuclear ambitions unresolved, Lebanon destabilized, terrorism risks rising, and a shadow war between Israel and Iran poised to reignite at any moment. The most likely scenarios moving forward involve either Iran exerting more control over global energy markets than before the fighting, or the current tenuous agreement merely delaying another military escalation by days or weeks, according to geopolitical and energy experts.
🏗️
Bushehr Nuclear Plant Strikes Risk Regional Radioactive Catastrophe
Iran's Foreign Minister claimed the Bushehr facility had been "bombed" four times since the war erupted on February 28, criticizing what he called a "lack of concern" for nuclear safety on the part of the United States and Israel. A strike on a nuclear reactor or storage pools could cause the release of Caesium-137 into the atmosphere. IAEA Director-General Grossi previously warned an Israeli strike on Bushehr could trigger a regional catastrophe and "result in a very high release of radioactivity" with "great consequences" beyond Iran's borders.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸
United States
White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt said the United States has "achieved and exceeded" its core military objectives against Iran during Operation Epic Fury. The U.S. military stated it remains prepared to resume attacks if ordered. "A ceasefire is a pause, and the joint force remains ready, if ordered or called upon," Gen. Caine said.
🇮🇱
Israel
Netanyahu said the U.S. and Israel have "achieved tremendous accomplishments" in Iran, including destroying Iran's missile production capabilities and stopping its nuclear development. Netanyahu said the ceasefire would not extend to its fighting with Hezbollah or Israel's invasion of southern Lebanon. The IAF said it launched its "largest attack across Lebanon" since Operation Roaring Lion began, completing strikes targeting approximately 100 command centers and military infrastructures of Hezbollah across Beirut, the Bekaa, and southern Lebanon.
🇮🇷
Iran
Iranian leaders touted the ceasefire as a victory, noting they believed the U.S. agreed to "the general framework" of Iran's 10-point proposal. "Good news to the dear nation of Iran! Nearly all the objectives of the war have been achieved," the Supreme National Security Council said. Foreign Minister Araghchi stated: "The Iran–U.S. ceasefire terms are clear and explicit: the U.S. must choose — ceasefire or continued war via Israel. It cannot have both. The world sees the massacres in Lebanon. The ball is in the U.S. court."
🇵🇰
Pakistan (Mediator)
Pakistani PM Shehbaz Sharif, key broker of the ceasefire, posted: "Both parties have displayed remarkable wisdom and understanding and have remained constructively engaged in furthering the cause of peace and stability." Sharif subsequently called out violations of the agreement and urged "all parties to exercise restraint and respect the ceasefire for two weeks, as agreed upon, so that diplomacy can take a lead role."
🇷🇺🇨🇳
Russia & China
Russia and China condemned U.S.-Israeli attacks from the start but stopped short of providing military assistance to Iran. Chinese FM Wang Yi condemned the "outrageous assassination of a sovereign leader" (Khamenei). Russia's FM Lavrov warned the attacks could accelerate Iranian nuclear proliferation — the very outcome the attackers claimed to prevent. China and Russia vetoed a U.N. Security Council resolution aimed at reopening the Strait of Hormuz.
🇬🇧
United Kingdom
British Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper called Israel's recent strikes on Lebanon "deeply damaging," saying: "We want to see Lebanon included in the ceasefire... That escalation that we saw from Israel yesterday was deeply damaging, and we want to see an end to hostilities." Earlier, UK PM Keir Starmer said UK forces were engaged in "coordinated regional defensive operations" and called on Iran to "give up their weapons programmes."
🇪🇺
European Union
EU Commission President von der Leyen and EU Council President Costa called the conflict "greatly concerning" and urged all parties to "exercise maximum restraint, to protect civilians, and to fully respect international law." MEP Marc Botenga (The Left) was a pointed EU critic, telling Euronews the EU had made "no diplomatic efforts whatsoever" and accusing it of failing to condemn what he called "genocidal rhetoric" from Trump.
🇸🇦
Saudi Arabia
According to The Washington Post and The New York Times, Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman had multiple phone calls with Trump urging him to attack Iran, and Trump's decision came after the Saudi and Israeli governments lobbied him repeatedly. Saudi Arabia has since been struck by Iranian drones and missiles and is now participating in ceasefire diplomacy, with the Saudi and Iranian FMs speaking officially for the first time since the war began on April 9.
🇮🇹🇪🇸
Italy, Spain & Others
Italy, Spain, and China joined a chorus of condemnations against Israel's Lebanon strikes on April 9, saying they constituted a truce violation.
🇺🇳
United Nations
UN Secretary-General António Guterres and several uninvolved countries condemned the U.S.-Israeli strikes; the UN Security Council later passed a resolution condemning Iran's retaliatory strikes on Gulf states. UN human rights chief Volker Türk denounced the "tirade of incendiary rhetoric" being used by all parties in relation to the escalating conflict.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

As of April 9, 2026, the most critical uncertainties center on whether the fragile two-week ceasefire will hold. Iran has informed mediators it will only participate in Islamabad talks if the ceasefire covers Lebanon, and has threatened to reverse its decision to reopen the Strait of Hormuz if Israel continues striking Hezbollah. Key sticking points include Iran's claimed right to uranium enrichment — included in its 10-point proposal — versus Leavitt's statement that this is "a red line that the President is not going to back away from."

Former intelligence officer Brandan Buck (Cato Institute) cautioned: "A ceasefire between who? It could be durable between the U.S. and Iran, but as far as the larger region, it's not looking that way. Iran devolved its command-and-control authority down to the lowest levels throughout the conflict — if this is going to hold, it will take time for those orders to filter down." Chatham House analysts noted: "This had become a war that no side was clearly winning and in which the costs were rising faster than any achievable gains." Key questions remain: Can the U.S. offer credible assurances against renewed strikes? And is Iran willing to accept limits on its ability to threaten Hormuz shipping?

Traffic for oil, LNG, fertilizer, and petrochemicals plunged to 5% of typical flows in March and only grew to nearly 10% for a few days in early April before ceasing again on April 8. Only a single Iranian-linked oil tanker passed through the strait on April 8. The EIA cautions that Middle East oil production will not return close to pre-conflict levels until late 2026.

Chatham House warned that "Israel's insistence that its military action in Lebanon is not part of the agreement reveals a key vulnerability and shows the limits of the U.S. ability to manage its allies: the ongoing bombing campaigns in Lebanon could undermine the ceasefire overall and keep the U.S. trapped in a conflict it is now seeking to exit."
