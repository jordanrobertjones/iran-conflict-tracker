📋 What Happened — Background

March 27, 2026

The strikes began on 28 February 2026, when Israel and the United States launched attacks on targets across Iran, codenamed Operation Roaring Lion in Israel and Operation Epic Fury in the United States. Khamenei was killed in the first wave of strikes on February 28, as were dozens of other top Iranian officials. Just before the strikes began, on 27 February 2026, Oman's Foreign Minister Badr Al-Busaidi said a "breakthrough" had been reached in diplomatic negotiations, but after the US and Israel attacked Iran, Al-Busaidi said that he was dismayed and that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined.

Since the operation began, approximately 5,700 combat sorties have been flown, with more than 540 strike waves conducted across central and western Iran and dozens more deep inside Iranian territory. Iran's death toll in the war has surpassed 1,500, according to its Ministry of Health, while 15 people have been killed in Israel by Iranian attacks. Approximately 2,000 vessels and 20,000 seafarers are stranded in the Strait of Hormuz, creating severe logistical and humanitarian challenges.

✅ Arguments For the War

Military Objectives Progressing: The IDF has claimed to have destroyed or disabled around 70 percent of Iran's estimated 500 ballistic missile launchers, and U.S. and Israeli airstrikes have neutralized over 70 percent of Iran's missile launchers and driven missile production to zero, according to Israeli intelligence estimates.
Regional Allies Face Reduced Threats: Hezbollah is "significantly weakened" according to analysis, and 82% of the Israeli public supported ongoing military operations, including 93% of Jewish Israelis.
Iran's Offensive Capability Declining: Iranian ballistic missile launches have fallen by more than 90 percent from 350 on February 28 to roughly 25 by March 14, and drone launches fell from more than 800 on Day 1 to about 75 on Day 15.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Civilian Casualties Mounting: Preliminary figures are 1,937 dead in Iran, at least 19 in Israel, 13 US soldiers and 25 killed in Gulf states, with 217 children killed in Iran and Israeli strikes on Lebanon have killed 1,116 people and injured 3,229 others since March 2, with at least 121 children and 42 health workers.
War Crimes Allegations: International human rights experts and U.N. officials say the warning to strike power plants is an open threat to possibly commit a war crime.
Domestic Opposition Growing: 59% of Americans think the US made the wrong decision in using military force in Iran, and 61% disapprove of Trump's handling of the conflict, with 64 percent disapproving of his handling of the Iran war in a Fox News poll, with only 36 percent approving.
Diplomatic Stalemate: Iran rejected the US 15-point ceasefire proposal on March 25, labeling it "one-sided," and issued counter-demands. Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi has refused to characterize the countries' dialogue as a negotiation, instead calling it an "exchange of messages".

🌍 How the World Has Responded

Israel

82% of the Israeli public supported ongoing military operations, including 93% of Jewish Israelis.

Germany

Germany's foreign minister, Johann Wadephul, has called for NATO allies to develop a "common position" with the US, though Norway's Foreign Minister stated the attack "is not in line with international law. Preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat."

UK/EU

The Prime Minister condemned Iranian counter-strikes and called for resumption of diplomacy, saying he did "not believe in regime change from the skies". EU Commission President Ursula von der Leyen called the conflict "greatly concerning" and urged restraint.

China & Russia

Russia and China condemning U.S. and Israeli actions. The EU accused Russia of providing intelligence support to Iran in the war to "kill Americans".

Gulf States

The Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) Secretary-General al-Budaiwi condemned Iran's military actions and its closure of the Strait of Hormuz, stating that Tehran has "overcome all red lines." In the Middle East, most countries criticized the Iranian attacks.

Hezbollah & Houthis

Hezbollah's Secretary-General Naim Qassem stated that the group is now in a war against both the US and Israel and will do everything it can to defend Lebanese territory. Yemen's Houthi leader warned of a "military response" should the war require it, saying "As the people of Yemen, we repay loyalty with loyalty".

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Trump has delayed planned attacks on Iran's energy infrastructure by 10 days until April 6, saying peace talks are going "very well", with mediators pushing for possible in-person talks between Iranians and Americans, perhaps as soon as this weekend in Pakistan. However, Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi has refused to characterize the countries' dialogue as a negotiation, instead calling it an "exchange of messages" and describing US overtures for talks as "an admission of defeat." Iran rejected the US 15-point ceasefire proposal on March 25, labeling it "one-sided," and issued counter-demands.

Critics raise the question: What prevents Iran from restarting production? The answer requires a post-conflict framework that does not yet exist in public: a verification regime, a diplomatic settlement or a sustained enforcement posture. The administration owes the American public and its regional partners a clear account of what that framework would look like.
