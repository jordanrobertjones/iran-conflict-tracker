📋 What Happened — Background

March 09, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury/Operation Roaring Lion, a joint military campaign against Iran. The opening strikes killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of top officials. The campaign has since expanded with over 3,000 US strikes on Iranian targets including military infrastructure, missile sites, and regime facilities. As of March 9, 2026, over 1,332 people have been killed in Iran according to the Iranian Red Crescent.

On March 8, 2026, Iran's Assembly of Experts named Mojtaba Khamenei, the 56-year-old son of the late Supreme Leader, as Iran's new supreme leader. Key political leaders, the IRGC, and armed forces have pledged allegiance to him. Trump called the selection "unacceptable" and said he must "be involved in the appointment." Israel has threatened to target any successor. The selection signals hardline continuity and little immediate prospect for negotiations.

The conflict has spread regionally with Hezbollah entering the war on March 2, launching strikes on Israel and prompting Israeli operations in Lebanon that have killed over 394 people and displaced 95,000. Iran has fired over 500 ballistic missiles and 2,000 drones since the war began, targeting Israel, US bases, and Gulf states. Eight US service members have been killed. The war has disrupted global travel, shut down major airports, and sent oil prices above $110/barrel.

✅ Arguments For the War

Degradation of Iranian military capability: US-Israeli strikes have destroyed Iran's navy (43 warships sunk), neutralized approximately 300 missile launchers, and established air superiority. Trump stated Iran has "no navy, air force, air detection, or radar" remaining.
Decapitation of regime leadership: The killing of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of top officials disrupted Iranian command and control. Iran's Foreign Ministry acknowledged the military "has lost control over several units."
High Israeli public support: A March 2026 Israel Democracy Institute survey found 82% of the Israeli public supports ongoing military operations, including 93% of Jewish Israelis.
Regional alignment: Gulf states have cooperated with US defensive operations despite being targeted by Iranian retaliation. UK has pledged military support including fighter jets and a destroyer to defend Saudi Arabia.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Civilian casualties and potential war crimes: More than 160 people, mostly children, were killed when a strike hit a girls' school near Bandar Abbas. Evidence suggests US involvement despite denials. The UN Special Rapporteur stated the attacks are "widely considered illegal according to international law."
Regional escalation: The conflict has spread to Lebanon with over 394 killed. Iran has struck US embassies in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait, hit Dubai International Airport, and targeted oil infrastructure across the Gulf. Azerbaijan has announced retaliatory measures after being struck.
Economic damage: Oil prices have spiked to over $114/barrel—the highest since COVID-19. The Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed. Qatar's energy minister warned continued conflict "will bring down economies of the world." The war costs the US approximately $891 million per day.
Unclear endgame: Experts note shifting US war aims and no clear exit strategy. ACLED analysts assess "the war's trajectory may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated." Only 25% of Americans support the attacks according to Reuters/IPSOS.
Hardliner succession: The appointment of Mojtaba Khamenei as new supreme leader signals "continuity" with hardline factions retaining power, indicating "little desire to agree to a deal or negotiations in the short term."

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States: Trump demands Iran's "unconditional surrender" and threatens the new supreme leader "won't last long" without US approval.
🇮🇱 Israel: Netanyahu vows to strike Iran "with all our might." IDF threatened to "pursue every successor" to Khamenei. 82% public support for operations.
🇬🇧 United Kingdom: Starmer permitted use of British bases for "defensive purposes" and pledged military support to Saudi Arabia. Opposition leader Badenoch "stands with our allies." Liberal Democrats called action "unilateral and illegal."
🇫🇷 France: Authorized US use of French bases. Called for Iran to "immediately cease strikes." Deploying aircraft carrier to Mediterranean.
🇷🇺 Russia: Putin condemned strikes as "cynical violation of all norms." Providing intelligence to Iran. Pledged "unwavering" support for new supreme leader.
🇨🇳 China: Urged both sides to stop military operations. Sent envoy to Middle East. Opposes any targeting of new Supreme Leader.
🇪🇸 Spain: Denied use of military bases for US operations connected to offensive against Iran.
🇦🇿 Azerbaijan: Initially sought neutrality but after being struck by Iranian drones, President Aliyev ordered armed forces to "prepare and implement appropriate retaliatory measures."
Gulf States: Cooperating with US defense despite being targeted. Saudi Crown Prince vowed military force against Iranian incursions. Iranian President Pezeshkian apologized for attacks on neighbors.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Duration of conflict: White House projects 4-6 weeks, but Pentagon declines to give timeline. Brookings' Suzanne Maloney notes "this is going to be more complicated than the White House may have hoped." Israeli Army Chief said war will "likely continue for a long time."

Regime change prospects: Mojtaba Khamenei's appointment signals regime resilience. ACLED assesses "the Islamic Republic is more likely to continue fighting than to concede." Whether Trump accepts any outcome short of surrender remains unclear.

Economic impact: Oil at $114/barrel with potential for higher. Gulf states may be forced to halt exports. Oxford Economics warns of material economic damage from Strait of Hormuz disruption.

Ground troops: Defense Secretary Hegseth declined to rule out boots on the ground. Experts say ground troops may be required for regime change objectives. Public opposition makes this politically risky.
