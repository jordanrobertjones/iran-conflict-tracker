📋 What Happened — Background

April 01, 2026

On February 27, President Trump ordered Operation Epic Fury, and on February 28, US missiles, drones, and Israeli fighter jets began striking Iran. The strikes took place during active negotiations over Iran's nuclear program. Just before strikes began, Oman's Foreign Minister announced a diplomatic "breakthrough" with Iran agreeing to nuclear concessions, saying peace was "within reach," but after the US and Israel attacked Iran, he said active negotiations had been undermined.

As of April 1, 2026 (Day 33 of the conflict), the joint US-Israeli campaign has evolved from initial strikes that killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior military figures, into sustained, large-scale air operations across Iran. Unlike the June 2025 strikes, the current conflict has spread across at least a dozen countries, closed the Strait of Hormuz – the world's major oil artery – and killed more than 2,300 people in the region.

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio stated communication between Tehran and Washington is ongoing, mostly through intermediaries, with US war objectives to be achieved in "weeks, not months". Iran's President stated his country is ready to stop fighting provided it receives guarantees against future attacks.

✅ Arguments For the War

Degradation of Iranian Military Capabilities: U.S. and Israeli airstrikes have neutralized over 70 percent of Iran's missile launchers and driven missile production to zero, according to Israeli intelligence estimates. Israel's primary objectives have been to degrade Iran's missile capabilities by targeting launchers, production sites, and underground infrastructure; as launch facilities and storage depots are struck, Iran's ability to regenerate and coordinate missile fire is likely to weaken.
Elimination of Regime Leadership: The campaign has decapitated much of Iran's top military and IRGC leadership, including Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and other senior commanders.
Reduced Nuclear Proliferation Risk (Short-term): U.S. and Israeli strikes may have significantly reduced the risk of short-term Iranian proliferation.
Support from Arab Gulf States: A UN Security Council resolution condemning Iran's attacks received co-sponsorship from 135 countries, reportedly the largest number of countries ever to cosponsor a Security Council draft resolution.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Massive Civilian Casualties: By the end of the first week of the war, US and Israeli attacks had killed 1,332 people in Iran, including a bombing of a girls' school in Minab that killed more than 170 people, mostly children. The NGO HRANA documented 3,114 deaths in Iran due to airstrikes by March 17, including 1,354 civilians.
Escalation and Regional Spread: In the weeks that followed, the conflict escalated with a velocity and intensity that surprised even senior analysts; by Day 11, Defense Secretary Hegseth was publicly declaring each successive day would be "our most intense day of strikes inside Iran". Violence in Lebanon displaced hundreds of thousands of people and killed hundreds.
Blockade of Strait of Hormuz: Shipping through the Strait of Hormuz slowed to a standstill, with 150 freight ships, including many oil tankers, stalled. State media reported Iran's parliament approved tolls on vessels transiting the Strait, a key oil and gas route effectively closed by the war.
Global Economic Impact: Oil prices surpassed $112 per barrel, the highest since 2022, amid supply fears. The UN World Food Programme and various economic analysts have warned that the war is driving significant, long-term increases in global food prices.
New Proliferation Risks from Conflict: Iran still possesses 400 kilograms of 60 percent enriched uranium, and the exact location remains unknown; Iran's specialized nuclear and missile scientists may be scattered by the conflict, posing potential proliferation risks.
Diplomatic Opportunity Squandered: Secretary-General António Guterres noted that opportunities for diplomacy with Iran over its nuclear program had been "squandered".
Regime Has Not Collapsed: Despite the decapitation of its leadership, the Iranian government did not collapse and Iran did not see any major defections or antigovernment protests.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

US/Israel Position

Israel said "Targeting civilians is wrong, targeting cities is wrong, and Iran must stop," stressing that Iran used diplomacy as cover while it fortified its nuclear programme. The US noted that President Trump will not allow "fake negotiations" to be used as a "delay tactic" by Iranian leaders.

Arab Gulf States

Gulf states condemned the Iranian attacks as a violation of their sovereignty, stressing that they have been neutral in the war. The UN Security Council condemned Iran's attacks against Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, the UAE and Jordan, and demanded immediate cessation of attacks on civilian objects.

European Allies

The five European Council members – France, Denmark, Greece, Latvia and the UK – all framed the crisis by placing the burden of blame on Iran for its nuclear activities and January crackdown on protesters, while passing over the legality of the U.S.-Israeli strikes without comment. 10 European foreign ministers urged Israel to avoid further widening of conflict in Lebanon, warned that an expanded operation would risk broader regional crisis, but placed primary blame on Hezbollah for reigniting hostilities.

China & Russia Position

China's ambassador told the council that the conflict had "neither legitimacy nor legal basis" and the US and Israel must cease their attacks. Russia said the resolution condemning Iran was "expressed in a biased and one-sided tone," and without context would lead one to believe Tehran decided to strike targets out of pure malice for no reason. Both countries abstained on UN Security Council resolution 2817 condemning Iran's attacks.

Pakistan, Turkey, Egypt Mediation

Ministers from Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, Turkey and Egypt met in Islamabad to come up with a plan to de-escalate the war; Pakistan offered to host talks between the US and Iran. China and Pakistan agreed on a five-point proposal calling for an immediate halt to hostilities, launch of peace talks, protection of non-military sites, safeguarding navigation through the Strait of Hormuz, and upholding the UN Charter.

Oman's Nuanced Position

Oman's Foreign Minister described the Iranian attacks as "completely unacceptable" but "probably the only rational option" to the US and Israeli strikes.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

The trajectory of the conflict remains highly uncertain. With its leadership decapitated, strikes at the heart of its military apparatus, and a wider regional conflagration ignited, the Islamic Republic is fighting for its survival; the ultimate outcome remains profoundly uncertain, and despite unprecedented military intensity, full capitulation remains unlikely even after Khamenei's killing.

The absence of a coherent Iranian interlocutor with authority to negotiate creates risk of uncontrolled escalation; Iran's distributed proxy network across Iraq, Syria, Yemen, and Lebanon can be activated incrementally, and each proxy attack creates political pressure on Washington to respond, producing a feedback loop difficult to break without decisive military victory or collapse of will.

By the end of the first week of the war, it was clear that the Iranian regime had not collapsed and that the conflict was not going to be a brief, one-and-done operation.
