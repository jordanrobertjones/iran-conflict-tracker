📋 What Happened — Background

March 31, 2026

On February 27, Omani foreign minister Badr Al-Busaidi announced a "breakthrough" in talks where Iran agreed to never stockpile enriched uranium and allow full IAEA verification, with peace described as "within reach." However, after the US and Israel attacked Iran, Al-Busaidi said he was dismayed that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined. The strikes began on February 28, 2026, when Israel and the United States launched attacks on targets across Iran, codenamed Operation Roaring Lion in Israel and Operation Epic Fury in the United States. The current campaign has damaged the Natanz nuclear facility, the one in Fordow remains inoperable, and defense industrial facilities that would be needed to reconstitute enrichment capacity are being systematically targeted. More than 9,000 targets have been struck since the launch of Operation Epic Fury, according to a March 23, 2026, fact sheet from U.S. Central Command, alongside more than 9,000 combat flights.

✅ Arguments For the War

Degraded Iranian Capabilities: US Secretary of State Marco Rubio stated US war objectives would be achieved in "weeks, not months" and that the Strait would remain open, with the US having destroyed Iran's air force and much of its navy. U.S. and Israeli airstrikes have neutralized over 70 percent of Iran's missile launchers and driven missile production to zero, while eliminating most of Iran's defense industrial base and setting back several key Iranian defense programs by years.
Regional Deterrence Signaling: A March 2026 survey by the Israel Democracy Institute found that 82% of the Israeli public supported ongoing military operations, including 93% of Jewish Israelis.
Houthi Entry Contained: Israel intercepted two drones from Yemen after Iranian-backed Houthi rebels waded into the conflict for the first time on Monday.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Regime Resilience & Escalation: Iran's parliament speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf accused the US of "secretly planning a ground invasion" while floating negotiations, and said Tehran's forces are "waiting for them." A solid 51 percent of academic experts expected the Iranian regime to survive in something like its prewar form.
Expanding Regional Conflict: Foreign ministers from Pakistan, Turkiye, Egypt and Saudi Arabia are scheduled to hold talks in Islamabad aimed at ending the war, as the conflict has escalated across the Middle East with Yemen's Houthis joining the war by firing missiles at Israel. Israel is fighting this conflict on three fronts – Iran, Hezbollah in Lebanon and the Houthis in Yemen.
Economic & Humanitarian Crisis: The US benchmark for crude oil breached $100 a barrel for the first time since 2022, with average petrol prices at bunkers crossing $4 a gallon for the first time since Russia's war on Ukraine. At least 1,937 people have been killed by US-Israeli attacks on Iran since February 28, Iran's Health Ministry says.
Uncertain Endgame & Objectives Mismatch: What is less clear is how well the Israeli goal of regime change matches the United States' objectives, as oil prices spike, markets dip, and shipping is disrupted, making it possible to imagine Trump seeking an earlier off-ramp. Most experts remained deeply pessimistic about the war producing an enduring setback to the Iranian nuclear program, with three times as many (21 percent) expecting the war to result in Iran accelerating its nuclear program rather than setting it back for the long term.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇮🇱 Israel
Strongly Supportive: Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu stated "for 47 years, the Ayatollah regime has chanted 'Death to Israel'," while Defense Minister Israel Katz said "he who acted to destroy Israel – has been destroyed... Justice has been served." On 29 March, Israeli official Yuli Edelstein said the strikes had set Iran back by years, but they had not yet succeeded in removing the Iranian threat.
🇺🇸 United States (Trump Admin)
Fully Supportive: President Donald Trump threatened to "completely obliterate" all of Iran's energy sources if a deal isn't reached to end the war. On 30 March, Trump said on Truth Social that if a deal was not reached soon and the Strait of Hormuz reopened, the US would destroy all of Iran's power plants, oil wells, Kharg Island, and possibly all its desalinization plants.
🇮🇷 Iran
Strongly Opposed: Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baghaei said there are currently no direct negotiations between the US and Iran, with messages only relayed through intermediaries. Iran's parliament speaker said the US was sending messages about possible negotiations while also planning a ground invasion, with Tehran ready to respond if US soldiers were deployed.
🇬🇧 UK
Critical of Strikes, Diplomatic Focus: In a joint statement with the leaders of France and Germany, Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer condemned Iranian counter-strikes and called for resumption of diplomacy, reiterating shared positions on Iran's nuclear program, while stating he did "not believe in regime change from the skies."
🌍 Global Community
Mixed - Most Call for Peace: Most countries did not take a definitive side but called for peace; in the Middle East, most criticized Iranian attacks; in Europe, the majority condemned both sides or called for peace. Norway's Foreign Minister said the initial attack breached international law standards, stating "the attack is not in line with international law" as "preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat."

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Duration & Endgame Unclear: Israel and the United States originally set a goal for the operation to last around 72 hours, hoping to prevent a lengthy conflict, but wars in this region often do not stick to predicted timelines, with the key issue being whether they achieve clear and meaningful goals and whether Washington and Jerusalem share a common vision of what those goals are. A senior Iranian security official told CNN that Tehran will determine when the war ends, stating "This is our war, and we will not stop defending until we teach Trump and Netanyahu a historic lesson."

Nuclear Program Trajectory Unknown: The International Atomic Energy Agency said that while Iran has an "ambitious" nuclear program and has refused to allow inspections of its damaged sites since the 2025 war, there was no evidence of a structured nuclear weapons program when the 2026 war began. Nearly half of academic respondents (45 percent) had some confidence that a war would at least temporarily set back the Iranian nuclear program, but only a small minority (6 percent) expected a long-term setback, with only 9 percent predicting a long-term setback after observing opening days of the war.
