📋 What Happened — Background

March 06, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated military strikes against Iran, codenamed Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The attacks killed Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and numerous senior officials. As of Day 7 (March 6), the conflict has expanded significantly across the region.

US CENTCOM reports B-2 bombers have dropped dozens of 2,000-pound bombs on Iranian missile launchers, contributing to a 90% reduction in Iranian missile attacks. The US has struck or sunk over 20 Iranian naval vessels, including the IRIS Dena frigate torpedoed off Sri Lanka—the first submarine combat sinking since the Falklands War. Israel reports over 2,500 strikes with more than 6,000 weapons deployed.

Iran has retaliated with Operation True Promise IV, striking US bases across the Gulf, as well as targets in Israel, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and reportedly Azerbaijan. The Strait of Hormuz has been declared closed by the IRGC, disrupting approximately 20% of global oil supplies. Death toll in Iran exceeds 1,200 according to state media, with more than 120 killed in Lebanon. The WHO has identified 13 Iranian health infrastructure sites struck.

Israel has launched a ground invasion of Lebanon and evacuated southern Beirut. Kurdish-Iranian armed groups have reportedly launched a ground offensive in northwest Iran. The US Senate defeated a War Powers Resolution, allowing strikes to continue without explicit congressional authorization.

✅ Arguments For the War

✓
Degraded Iranian military capability

US reports 90% reduction in Iranian missile attacks; Iran's navy described as "at the bottom of the sea"; over 300 missile launchers neutralized

✓
Elimination of regime leadership

Supreme Leader Khamenei, military chief of staff, and over 40 senior officials killed; IRGC command structure severely disrupted

✓
Nuclear program disruption

CSIS notes strikes may have "significantly reduced the risk of short-term Iranian proliferation"; nuclear-related facilities targeted

✓
Allied support emerging

UK providing defensive support; France authorized use of bases; Canada expressed conditional support; Qatar conducted retaliatory strikes against Iran

✓
Iranian regime at historic low

January 2026 protests showed weakened domestic legitimacy; regional allies Hamas and Hezbollah significantly degraded since 2023

❌ Arguments Against the War

✗
Regional escalation and civilian harm

Conflict spread to Lebanon, Iraq, Gulf states; over 1,200 killed in Iran including children at a school; UNESCO site damaged; WHO reports 13 health facilities struck

✗
International law concerns

UN experts condemned strikes as violating UN Charter; Italy's defense minister said attack "violated international law"; Spain refused base access saying "No to war"

✗
Severe economic disruption

Strait of Hormuz closed affecting 20% of global oil; Brent crude spiked from $70 to $80+; South Korea's KOSPI crashed 12%; QatarEnergy halted LNG production

✗
Constitutional concerns

Stimson Center: "unconstitutional" war launched "without congressional approval, without serious public debate"; 60% of Americans disapprove per CNN poll

✗
Unclear endgame risks prolonged conflict

Brookings: "what we're seeing is going to be more complicated than the White House may have hoped"; experts say regime change requires ground troops administration reluctant to deploy

✗
Nuclear proliferation risks

CSIS warns 400kg of 60% enriched uranium location unknown; scattered Iranian nuclear scientists could pose proliferation risks to non-state actors

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States

Trump: "Our objective is to defend the American people by eliminating imminent threats." Senate defeated War Powers Resolution. Defense Secretary warns "just wait" for escalation.

🇮🇱 Israel

Netanyahu: "This is not an endless war. This will usher in an era of peace." Ground invasion of Lebanon launched March 3. Broad political consensus including opposition.

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

PM Starmer: UK forces "active" in "coordinated regional defensive operations." Permitted US use of bases for defensive purposes. RAF base in Cyprus struck by Iranian drone.

🇫🇷 France

Authorized US use of French bases (March 5). Sent aircraft carrier to Mediterranean. Macron called for urgent UNSC meeting, warning escalation "dangerous for everyone."

🇨🇦 Canada

PM Carney: "Canada supports the United States acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon." Not militarily engaged but maintains regional liaison presence.

🇦🇷 Argentina

President Milei: Khamenei was "one of the most evil, violent, and cruel individuals that human history has ever seen." Strong support for operation.

🇪🇸 Spain

PM Sánchez: "No to war." Denied use of military bases for US operations. Trump threatened to cut all trade with Spain in response.

🇨🇴 Colombia

Outright condemnation of US and Israeli airstrikes.

🇮🇷 Iran

Interim Leadership Council formed under Ali Larijani. Declared Strait of Hormuz closed. Threatened to "set fire" to any ship passing through. Strikes across Gulf region.

🇶🇦 Qatar

FM demanded "immediate halt" to strikes; said Iran trying to drag neighbors into war "not theirs." Shot down Iranian jets; evacuating residents near US Embassy.

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia

Condemned Iranian attacks "in strongest terms"; Crown Prince MBS reportedly urged Trump to attack Iran. No direct military participation announced.

🇹🇷 Turkey

NATO systems intercepted Iranian missile headed toward Turkish airspace. FM Fidan leading "diplomatic push" for ceasefire. Not taking sides.

🇮🇹 Italy

Defense minister said US-Israel attack "violated international law" even while sending defensive weapons to Gulf.

🇪🇺 European Union

Called conflict "greatly concerning"; urged "maximum restraint." Atlantic Council notes EU faces "strategic vacuum" with internal divisions.

🇺🇳 United Nations

Secretary-General: strikes "undermine international peace and security." IAEA chief urged return to diplomacy. Human rights experts condemned strikes as violating UN Charter.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Conflict duration: Trump claims 4-5 week timeline; experts warn regime change would require ground troops and could become protracted. Brookings notes operation "going to be more complicated than the White House may have hoped."

Regime change prospects: Conflicting administration messaging—Trump called for regime change, Hegseth walked it back. CSIS warns air strikes alone "cannot topple a government." Reports of US efforts to build opposition coalition including Kurdish groups.

Nuclear material: IAEA confirmed no nuclear facilities hit, but CSIS notes Iran still possesses 400kg of 60% enriched uranium with unknown location, posing proliferation risks if regime collapses.

Strait of Hormuz: IRGC declared closure, 150 ships stalled, but Trump claims Iran's navy is "at the bottom of the sea" and strait will remain open. Insurance industry awaiting details on US convoy escort proposal.

Ground troops: US expressed reluctance but Hegseth has not ruled out deployment. Experts say required for stated objectives. Kurdish ground offensive reportedly underway in northwest Iran.
