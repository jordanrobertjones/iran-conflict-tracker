📋 What Happened — Background

March 24, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated strikes against Iran under Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The opening salvo killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior officials. His son Mojtaba Khamenei has since been appointed as his successor.

As the conflict enters Day 25, over 8,000 US combat flights have been flown and more than 50,000 American troops are deployed across the Middle East. Iran has retaliated with missiles and drones against Israel, US bases, and Gulf states including Saudi Arabia, UAE, Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, and Oman. Israel has also launched a ground incursion into Lebanon against Hezbollah.

March 23-24 developments: President Trump announced a five-day pause on strikes against Iran's power grid amid claimed "productive conversations" about ending the war. Iran's Foreign Ministry denies any negotiations are taking place. Meanwhile, "unprecedented" Israeli strikes continue hitting Tehran. The IEA warns the global economy faces a "major, major threat" worse than the 1970s oil crises, with oil reaching $114/barrel before Trump's pause announcement. Over 3,000 vessels remain stranded in the Persian Gulf due to the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz.

✅ Arguments For the War

Eliminated Iranian regime leadership: Supreme Leader Khamenei and dozens of senior IRGC officials killed in initial strikes, creating potential for internal political change.
Degraded military capabilities: U.S. CENTCOM reports Iran's air force, navy, and air defenses have been significantly degraded or destroyed after 8,000+ targets struck.
Nuclear program setback: Operation continues strikes against remaining nuclear infrastructure following June 2025's Operation Midnight Hammer, which had already destroyed Fordow and Natanz enrichment facilities.
Weakened proxy network: Hezbollah and other Iran-backed groups fighting for survival; RAND analysts note Iran's "forward defense" doctrine is reaching its limits.
Strong Israeli public support: Israel Democracy Institute poll shows 82% of Israeli public supports ongoing military operations.
Some Gulf states now favor neutralizing Iran: Reuters reports that after Iranian attacks on their territories, some Gulf countries are now urging US to neutralize Iran "for good."

❌ Arguments Against the War

Significant civilian casualties: At least 1,047-1,500 Iranian civilians killed including 200+ children per HRANA; WHO reports 13 Iranian health facilities struck. A girls' school strike in Minab killed 160+ schoolchildren.
Global economic crisis: IEA head warns situation is "worse than the combined oil crises of 1973 and 1979"; at least 40 energy facilities across 9 countries damaged; Strait of Hormuz effectively closed with 3,000+ vessels stranded.
War cost escalating: US costs estimated at $18 billion by March 19; Pentagon requested additional $200 billion.
Regional destabilization: Conflict has spread to Lebanon (1,000+ killed since March 2), with Israeli ground incursion; Gulf states under sustained attack despite not participating in strikes.
Diplomatic failure: War launched days after Oman's foreign minister announced a diplomatic "breakthrough" was "within reach." Stimson Center experts call the war "unconstitutional, unwise."
Low US public support: Only 25% of Americans support the strikes per Reuters/Ipsos poll; just 5% of Democrats and 17% of Republicans support regime change.
No clear endgame: Experts warn Trump's goals have shifted repeatedly; Carnegie's Sadjadpour says what "began as a war of choice... has morphed into a war of necessity" without clear exit strategy.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇮🇱 Israel: PM Netanyahu: "for 47 years, the Ayatollah regime has chanted 'Death to Israel'... it has spilled our blood." 82% public support.
🇨🇦 Canada: PM Carney: "Canada supports the United States acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon."
🇺🇦 Ukraine: President Zelenskyy: "Whenever there is American resolve, global criminals weaken."
🇦🇷 Argentina: President Milei: "Iran is our enemy and we will win this war."
🇬🇧 UK: PM Starmer: "does not believe in regime change from the skies." Joint statement with France/Germany condemned Iranian counter-strikes but did not endorse US-Israeli operation. UK allowing use of bases for defensive operations.
🇫🇷 France: Macron: France "cannot approve of" strikes conducted "outside of international law," while condemning Iranian retaliation. Deployed Rafale jets for defensive action.
🇷🇺 Russia: FM Lavrov called attacks "reckless" and "premeditated, unprovoked act of armed aggression." Warned the war could paradoxically spur nuclear proliferation. Providing intelligence support to Iran.

🇪🇸 Spain: Denied US use of military bases; PM Sánchez called war a "big error." Withdrew ambassador from Israel.
🇳🇴 Norway: FM Eide: "The attack... is not in line with international law. Preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat."
🇺🇳 UN Experts: Human rights experts condemned attacks as violating "the fundamental prohibition on the use of force" under UN Charter; characterized school strike as potential war crime.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Will negotiations succeed? Trump claims "productive conversations" are underway; Iran denies any talks. Backchannel efforts via Pakistan, Turkey, and Egypt are confirmed by NPR.

How long will the conflict last? Trump originally said 4 weeks, but experts warn the war could become protracted. Carnegie's Sadjadpour: "I don't think President Trump is going to simply be able to end the war and claim victory."

Will the Strait of Hormuz reopen? CENTCOM says it's "physically open" but ships avoiding it due to Iranian attacks. Over 3,000 vessels stranded. Resolution critical for global economy.

What happens to Iran's leadership? Mojtaba Khamenei appointed Supreme Leader, but Israel killed Ali Larijani (SNSC head) on March 17. Regime survival uncertain but not imminent.

Will Russia/China intervene further? Both providing intelligence and material support but avoiding direct military involvement. Analysts see them as strategic "winners" benefiting from US distraction.
