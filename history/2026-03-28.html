📋 What Happened — Background

March 28, 2026

On February 28, 2026, U.S. and Israeli forces launched Operation Epic Fury, numbering nearly 900 strikes in the first 12 hours, with Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei killed in the first wave of strikes. Iran had 440kg of uranium enriched to 60 percent purity – enough, if further enriched, for as many as 10 nuclear weapons. Just before the strikes, on February 27, Oman's Foreign Minister reported a "breakthrough" with Iran agreeing to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full IAEA verification, with talks expected to resume on March 2. After the attack, Oman's Foreign Minister said he was dismayed and that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined.

✅ Arguments For the War

Nuclear Program Degradation: The current campaign has damaged the Natanz nuclear facility, Fordow remains inoperable, and defense industrial facilities needed to reconstitute enrichment capacity are being systematically targeted.
Military Infrastructure Destruction: U.S. and Israeli airstrikes have neutralized over 70 percent of Iran's missile launchers and driven missile production to zero, and have eliminated most of Iran's defense industrial base, setting back several key Iranian defense programs by years. Since the commencement of Operation Epic Fury, 92% of the large ships in the Iranian Navy have been eliminated.
Successful Air Defense: More than 90 percent of projectiles launched at Israeli territory have been intercepted, preserving strategic freedom of action.
Regional Security Benefits: Washington's stated goals of degrading Iran's nuclear program, ballistic missiles, navy, drones, and proxy control are being achieved, with more than fifty Iranian naval vessels on the sea floor.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Escalating Civilian Casualties: The war has claimed more than 1,500 lives in Iran and over 1,000 in Lebanon, along with 16 in Israel and 13 US military personnel, with unspecified numbers of civilians killed in the Gulf region and millions displaced.
Economic and Energy Crisis: The head of the International Energy Agency warned that the situation in the Middle East is "very severe" and is worse than the two energy crises of the 1970s put together. On March 18, 2026, tit-for-tat strikes on critical energy infrastructure escalated dangerously, with Israeli drones targeting Iran's Asaluyeh complex and Iranian missiles hitting Qatar's Ras Laffan energy sector.
Strait of Hormuz Disruption: Trump gave Iran a 48-hour ultimatum to open the strait to all ships, threatening to "obliterate" Iran's power plants, and Tehran said it would completely close the strait, through which one-fifth of global oil supplies pass.
Uncertain War Objectives and Duration: Given that the Islamic Republic is more likely to continue fighting than to concede, the war's trajectory may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated.
Potential War Crime Allegations: The New York Times reported preliminary findings indicating the strike on a girls' school was conducted by the US with outdated targeting data, and UN human rights experts characterized it as a potential war crime under the Rome Statute.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

Supportive:

Canada supports the U.S. "acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon and to prevent its regime from further threatening international peace and security," stating Iran "must never be allowed to obtain or develop nuclear weapons".

A March 2026 survey found that 82% of the Israeli public supported ongoing military operations, including 93% of Jewish Israelis.

Critical:

Russia called the US-Israeli strikes "a pre-planned and unprovoked act" of aggression and accused Washington and Tel Aviv of falsely hyping threats to pursue regime change.

Norway stated that the attack "is not in line with international law" because "preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat".

Spain denied the US use of military bases for Iran strikes; Trump threatened to halt trade, but Sánchez maintained Spain's position, declaring the war "a big error".

Neutral/Balanced:

France, Germany, and the UK issued a joint statement emphasizing they urged Iran to end its nuclear program but made clear none participated in the strikes, and the statement did not explicitly support or denounce the strikes, though it condemned Iranian retaliation.

Pope Leo XIV renewed his appeal for a ceasefire on March 24, urging leaders to pursue dialogue "not with weapons — rather through dialogue".

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

It remains unclear how well the Israeli goal of regime change matches US objectives; as oil prices spike and Iran continues to inflict economic damage, Trump may seek an earlier off-ramp, and if such a gap between Israeli and US goals opens, expect Trump to determine when the war ends. Trump administration officials have offered diverse explanations for starting the war (pre-emption, nuclear prevention, regime change), while Iranian and US officials rejected claims that Iran had been preparing an attack. Nearly half of academic respondents had some confidence the war would temporarily set back the Iranian nuclear program, but only 6 percent expected a long-term setback before the war and 9 percent after, with most remaining deeply pessimistic about producing either a negotiated agreement or enduring nuclear setback.
