📋 What Happened — Background

March 05, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated military strikes against Iran in operations codenamed "Operation Epic Fury" (US) and "Operation Roaring Lion" (Israel). The attacks killed Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and targeted military commanders, nuclear facilities, missile sites, and government infrastructure across Tehran, Isfahan, Qom, Karaj, and Kermanshah.

By Day 6 (March 5), over 1,230 Iranians have been killed according to Al Jazeera. The US has deployed over 50,000 troops, two aircraft carriers, and conducted nearly 2,000 strikes on Iranian targets. Iran's naval capacity has been "effectively neutralized" according to US officials, including the sinking of the IRIS Dena frigate off Sri Lanka on March 4—the first ship sunk by a US submarine since World War II.

Iran has responded with Operation True Promise IV, launching over 500 ballistic missiles and 2,000 drones at Israel, US bases in Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Jordan. Six US service members have been killed in a drone strike in Kuwait. The conflict has spread to Lebanon, with Israel conducting strikes against Hezbollah in Beirut.

On March 4, the US Senate voted 53-47 against a War Powers resolution that would have required Congressional approval to continue the war. Iran has rejected negotiations, with security chief Ali Larijani stating Tehran "has prepared itself for a long war."

✅ Arguments For the War

Elimination of Iranian nuclear threat: US and Israeli officials claim the strikes have degraded Iran's nuclear program, which they assert could have enriched uranium to weapons-grade within two weeks.
Decapitation of hostile regime: The killing of Khamenei and senior IRGC officials removes leaders who supported proxy groups (Hamas, Hezbollah) and allegedly pursued nuclear weapons for decades.
Destruction of Iranian military capacity: According to CENTCOM, Iran's navy has been "effectively neutralized," 300+ missile launchers destroyed, and air defenses suppressed—US and Israeli jets now fly uncontested over Iranian territory.
Gulf state alignment: Iran's retaliatory strikes on UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait have pushed Gulf states toward supporting US-Israeli operations, with Saudi Arabia and UAE signaling willingness to respond militarily.
Deterrence of future threats: Supporters argue the operation demonstrates US willingness to act decisively against hostile regimes, potentially deterring other adversaries.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Violation of international law: UN experts and legal scholars have condemned the strikes as "an unequivocal violation of the UN Charter," noting no Security Council authorization and no imminent threat justifying preemptive action.
Civilian casualties: Over 1,230 killed in Iran including reports of 148 children killed in a school strike in Minab—which the UN called a "grave violation of humanitarian law."
Regional escalation: The conflict has spread to Lebanon, with 50+ killed in Israeli strikes; Gulf states under Iranian missile attacks; oil flows disrupted; and over 1 million people stranded by flight cancellations.
No exit strategy: Experts warn of a "forever war" risk. Brookings' Suzanne Maloney noted: "Iran is a huge country...trying to move towards some kind of interim solution is going to be extremely difficult."
Lack of domestic support: Only 25% of Americans support the attacks per Reuters/IPSOS polling. Congress was not consulted; critics call it unconstitutional.
Economic fallout: Oil prices spiking, stock markets plunging (Dow dropped 900+ points), and energy security concerns for US allies including Turkey (15% of gas from Iran).

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States (Trump Admin): "We will finish this on 'America first' conditions" — Defense Secretary Hegseth. Senate Republicans blocked War Powers resolution 53-47.
🇮🇱 Israel: Netanyahu vowed to continue striking Iran "with force." Israel warned any new supreme leader would be "an unequivocal target for elimination."
🇬🇧 United Kingdom: PM Starmer granted US permission to use British bases for "defensive" strikes. UK forces participating in "coordinated regional defensive operations."
🇨🇦 Canada: PM Carney expressed support, stating the process must end Iran's "ability to acquire a nuclear weapon" and "export terrorism."
🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia / 🇦🇪 UAE: Both condemned Iranian retaliatory strikes as "cowardly" and signaled willingness to respond militarily. UAE recalled ambassador from Iran.
🇨🇳 China: Ambassador Fu Cong called strikes "brazen," condemned use of force to settle disputes, called for respecting Iran's "sovereignty, security, and territorial integrity."
🇷🇺 Russia: Delivered "robust denunciation" at UN Security Council. Putin held emergency Security Council meeting.
🇰🇵 North Korea: State media condemned US-Israel "war of aggression" but notably did not report Khamenei's death.
🇺🇳 UN Secretary-General: Guterres condemned strikes as undermining international peace, said diplomacy was "squandered."
🇺🇳 UN Human Rights Experts: Called strikes "unlawful" and a violation of Article 2 of the UN Charter, the right to life, and sovereign equality.
🇩🇪 Germany: Chancellor Merz said strikes are "not without risk" but refrained from direct criticism; focused on "agenda for the day after."
🇪🇺 EU: Chief diplomat Kallas called situation "perilous," urged protection of civilians. E3 (UK/France/Germany) signaled support for "proportionate defensive measures."
🇶🇦 Qatar: Foreign minister demanded "immediate halt" to Iranian strikes on Gulf states, accused Iran of dragging neighbors into war.
🇪🇸 Spain: Denied US use of military bases, prompting Trump to threaten economic retaliation.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Duration of conflict: Trump projected 4-5 weeks, but experts warn of "forever war" risk given lack of exit strategy and Iran's vow to fight a "long war."

Regime change outcome: Mojtaba Khamenei (son of late Supreme Leader) emerging as successor candidate; analysts note air power alone has never produced regime change and stable successor state.

Nuclear status: IAEA reports no confirmed damage to nuclear facilities with nuclear material; Iran claims one site was hit. Location of 60% enriched uranium is unknown.

Ground troops: Pentagon has not ruled out US boots on the ground in Iran.

Kurdish involvement: Reports of Kurdish-Iranian groups launching ground offensive in northwest Iran; US reportedly asked Iraqi Kurds to assist cross-border operations.
