📋 What Happened — Background

April 06, 2026

On February 27, 2026, President Trump ordered Operation Epic Fury while traveling on Air Force One. At 3:38 p.m. EST on February 27, Trump gave the order to proceed; U.S. missiles, drones, and Israeli fighter jets began striking Iran the next day around 9:45 a.m. IRST. Israel codenamed its component Operation Roaring Lion. Trump announced the attacks in a Truth Social post at 2:00 AM EST, with no address to the media or public briefing to Congress beyond a notification to the Gang of Eight.

Just before the strikes began, Oman's Foreign Minister Badr Al-Busaidi said a "breakthrough" had been reached and Iran had agreed to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full IAEA verification, saying peace was "within reach." After the attack, Al-Busaidi said he was dismayed and that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined.

The Israeli Air Force said it struck 500 military targets in western and central Iran, including air defenses and missile launchers, using about 200 fighter jets — the largest combat sortie in its history. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was assassinated in an Israeli air attack on his compound on February 28; Iranian state media confirmed his death on March 1. Mojtaba Khamenei was elected on March 8, 2026 to replace his father as supreme leader, with Iran's top leaders pledging their allegiance to him.

In the hours that followed, Iran initiated large-scale retaliation, expanding the conflict beyond Iranian territory into a region-wide exchange. What began as a concentrated campaign has developed into a sustained regional war with an expanding set of targets, including economic and logistical infrastructure. Iran launched missiles and drones on Bahrain, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE. Shipping through the Strait of Hormuz slowed to a standstill, with 150 freight ships stalled.

Admiral Brad Cooper stated that the U.S. military had so far struck more than 8,000 Iranian military targets, including 130 vessels. Iranian authorities say that more than 2,000 people have been killed in U.S.-Israeli strikes since the start of the war on February 28. The conflict between Hezbollah and Israel escalated into the 2026 Lebanon war, killing more than 1,400 people — both militants and civilians — by early April.

Trump extended a deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz to April 6, and on April 1 said he would only consider a ceasefire after Iran reopened the strait. As of April 6, Iran says it has formulated its response to ceasefire proposals presented by intermediaries but will not engage in direct talks while U.S. and Israeli attacks escalate. U.S.-Israeli attacks on April 6 alone have killed at least 34 people across Iran, including at least six children. Trump appeared to set a new "Tuesday, 8:00 P.M. Eastern Time" deadline, while an Iranian official said the strait would stay closed until the country is "fully compensated" for war damages.

✅ Arguments For the War

✅
Nuclear threat degraded: The IAEA noted Iran has an "ambitious" nuclear program and has refused inspections of its damaged sites since the 2025 war. Netanyahu asserted that U.S.-Israeli strikes have eliminated Iran's ability to produce nuclear weapons and ballistic missiles, removing what he called "two existential threats" to Israel.
✅
Iranian military severely degraded: Joint U.S.-Israeli efforts eliminated as many as three-quarters of Iranian missile launchers; by the tenth day of the war, Iranian missile and drone attacks had dropped by more than 90%. U.S. forces confirmed Iran's naval capability in the Gulf of Oman has been neutralized, including the sinking of all 11 previously active warships.
✅
Decapitation of Iranian leadership: Iranian defense minister Aziz Nasirzadeh, IRGC commander Mohammad Pakpour, and Defense Council secretary Ali Shamkhani were killed; the IDF confirmed the deaths of seven Iranian security leaders. In late March, the NYT described Iran's leadership as paralyzed, with severely disrupted decision-making and communications infrastructure causing paranoia and internal power struggles.
✅
Iran's steel and industrial capacity hit: Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu said Israeli strikes have destroyed about 70 percent of Iran's steel production capacity, significantly hitting Tehran's ability to manufacture weapons.
✅
Gulf states rallying against Iran: UAE Ambassador Yousef Al Otaiba wrote in The Wall Street Journal that Iran cannot be allowed to hold the global economy hostage, calling for "a conclusive outcome" addressing Iran's nuclear capabilities, missiles, drones, terror proxies, and blockades.

❌ Arguments Against the War

❌
Strait of Hormuz blockade — largest energy crisis since the 1970s: Brent crude surpassed $100 per barrel on March 8 for the first time in four years, rising to $126 per barrel at its peak. The closure has been described as the largest disruption to the energy supply since the 1970s energy crisis. Rising energy, shipping, and fertilizer costs are already being felt in the U.S. and around the world, with sharper consequences expected to fall on poorer, import-dependent countries.
❌
No clear exit strategy: Analysts said Trump's prime-time address to the nation failed to provide clarity on an exit strategy, with Deutsche Bank's Jim Reid noting there was "no signal of the U.S. seeking an imminent off-ramp." Aaron David Miller of the Carnegie Endowment assessed: "This has been a tactical set of brilliant operations by the American military. But if the war ended today, it would be a strategic disaster."
❌
Civilian casualties and cultural destruction: As of March 27, Iran reports damage to at least 120 historical sites by U.S.-Israeli strikes. A BBC Verify analysis found a deadly attack on residential buildings in the town of Lamerd killed 21 people, including 4 children, using a U.S. Precision Strike Missile. U.S. strikes on the B1 bridge between Tehran and Karaj reportedly killed 8 people and wounded 95 others; experts assessed the strike as a possible war crime, as many Iranian families were gathered in the parks below to celebrate a holiday.
❌
War launched over disputed intelligence: Iranian and some U.S. officials rejected claims that Iran had been preparing an attack. The IAEA said there was no evidence of a structured nuclear weapons program when the 2026 war began. Experts noted Iran seemed focused on short- and medium-range missiles with a top range of 2,000 km — far short of the U.S. Three unnamed American officials told The New York Times that Trump exaggerated the immediacy of the threat.
❌
Highly enriched uranium unaccounted for: Aaron David Miller noted that the highly enriched uranium has not been retrieved, the regime not only survives but has demonstrated a capacity to undermine Gulf security, and Iran has "weaponized geography" by asserting control over the Strait of Hormuz.
❌
Threat to strikes on civilian energy infrastructure: Trump warned that if Iran doesn't open the Strait of Hormuz, the U.S. would bomb the country's civilian energy infrastructure — strikes that experts say would likely constitute a war crime under international law. International human rights experts and U.N. officials said the warning to strike power plants is an open threat to possibly commit a war crime.
❌
Houthis entering the war; second strait at risk: The Houthis have entered the war. Whether they choose to block Bab al-Mandab — a second critical maritime chokepoint — could have significant further consequences for the global economy.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸
United States
President Trump and Prime Minister Netanyahu announced operations with close coordination. Both vowed to prevent Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons and called for its fall, though without committing to topple it directly.
🇮🇱
Israel
Netanyahu characterized the operation as a joint effort and thanked Trump for his "historic leadership." Netanyahu asserted the strikes eliminated Iran's ability to produce nuclear weapons and ballistic missiles, removing two "existential threats" to Israel.
🇨🇦
Canada
Prime Minister Mark Carney said Canada stands with the Iranian people, "reaffirms Israel's right to defend itself," and supports the U.S. "acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon."
🇬🇧
United Kingdom
In a joint statement with France and Germany, PM Keir Starmer condemned Iranian counter-strikes and called for diplomacy, but said he did "not believe in regime change from the skies." The UK has not participated militarily in the strikes, but UK aircraft have been deployed in a defensive role to intercept missiles in Qatar, Jordan, Iraq, and Cyprus.
🇫🇷
France
The E3 (UK, France, and Germany) resolved to back, if needed, "proportionate military defensive measures" against drones and ballistic missiles, signaling the potential for direct involvement. President Macron said the idea of using force to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, as suggested by Trump, is "unrealistic," adding it would leave cargo ships vulnerable to Iranian attacks.
🇳🇴
Norway
Foreign Minister Espen Barth Eide said the initial attack on Iran by Israel breached international law: "The attack is described by Israel as a preventive strike, but it is not in line with international law."
🇮🇪
Ireland
PM Michael Martin said: "The use of force by the United States and Israel against Iran, and the subsequent retaliation by Iran across the region, undermine international peace and security," warning of grave consequences for civilians.
🇷🇺
Russia
Russia condemned the strikes on Natanz as a "blatant violation of international law." Russia and China abstained on the UN Security Council resolution condemning Iran's retaliatory strikes.
🇨🇳
China
China stated that the war should not have started, and also criticised Iran for attacking other countries in the area. Analysts note that once the war ends, China may decide to help Tehran rebuild militarily in return for oil.
🇺🇳
United Nations
UN Human Rights Chief Volker Türk said the "reckless" war is disproportionately impacting civilians in the Middle East and beyond. UN Secretary-General Guterres condemned U.S.-Israeli attacks on Iran during an emergency Security Council meeting.
🇸🇦
Saudi Arabia
According to The Washington Post, Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman had multiple phone calls with Trump urging him to attack Iran, and Trump's decision came after the Saudi and Israeli governments lobbied him repeatedly. However, Saudi Arabia has also been hit by Iranian drones and missiles throughout the conflict.
🇴🇲
Oman
Foreign Minister Badr Al-Busaidi said that "active and serious negotiations" mediated by his country were "yet again undermined" and urged the U.S. "not to get sucked in further."

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

While Iran has denied holding direct or indirect negotiations with the U.S. and rejected calls for a ceasefire, the two sides have acknowledged exchanging messages through intermediaries. It is unclear whether anything has resulted from the discussions, with thousands more U.S. troops arriving in the Middle East.

The IAEA said that while Iran has an "ambitious" nuclear program and has refused to allow inspections of its damaged sites since the 2025 war, there was no evidence of a structured nuclear weapons program when the 2026 war began. The IAEA has been unable to access the sites the U.S. bombed; since Trump withdrew from the Iran nuclear deal in 2018, experts lack confirmed independent information about the status of Iran's efforts.

CSIS analyst Daniel Byman outlined two paths to regime change in Iran: popular unrest leading to collapse (as a variant of the 1979 Islamic Revolution), or a military coup — though he noted the coup path is harder because the military is having difficulty communicating with itself.

Despite Trump's weekend threats, the president signaled he may be willing to pull U.S. forces out of the conflict in two to three weeks, even if the Strait of Hormuz is still effectively shut. Aaron David Miller warned that a three-month interruption of normal maritime commerce could drive oil prices, according to Bloomberg, in excess of $170 a barrel as the summer driving season approaches.
