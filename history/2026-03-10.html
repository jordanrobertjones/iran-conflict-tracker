📋 What Happened — Background

March 10, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched a coordinated military campaign against Iran—codenamed Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The stated objectives were to destroy Iran's ballistic missile infrastructure and nuclear program, eliminate IRGC leadership, neutralize Iran's naval capabilities, and promote regime change.

Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed on the first day of strikes. On March 8, 2026, his son Mojtaba Khamenei was appointed as Iran's new Supreme Leader by the Assembly of Experts, signaling continuity of hardline policies. Iran has responded with waves of missiles and drones targeting Israel and US bases across the Gulf region.

As of Day 11 (March 10, 2026): 8 US soldiers have been killed; Iran reports over 1,255 dead and 10,000 injured; Lebanon reports 394 killed (including 83 children) and 500,000+ displaced. Over 5,000 US-Israeli targets have been struck in Iran. Oil prices briefly surged past $120/barrel before settling around $90. US gas prices have risen ~50 cents/gallon since the war began.

Iran's IRGC claims it is deploying missiles with warheads weighing over 1 tonne, rejecting US claims that its missile program has been destroyed. Defense Secretary Hegseth announced Tuesday (March 11) will see "the most intense days of strikes" since the war began. Trump has called the war a "short-term excursion" that will end "pretty quickly," though gave no timeline.

✅ Arguments For the War

⚔️
Degradation of Iran's Military Capacity

Trump claims 80-90% of Iran's missile launchers destroyed; US asserts it has struck 5,000+ targets including air defenses, naval assets (17 warships reportedly destroyed), and missile infrastructure.

☢️
Nuclear Threat Reduction

CSIS analysts note US-Israeli strikes "may have significantly reduced the risk of short-term Iranian proliferation," though Iran still possesses 400 kg of 60% enriched uranium at unknown locations.

🌍
Allied Support

NATO Secretary General Rutte said Europe is "supportive" of US attacks. 82% of Israeli public supports the operation. France authorized US use of French bases. Australia is sending missiles to UAE.

📉
Weakened Iranian Proxies

Hezbollah faces intensified pressure with Lebanese government banning its military activities; the group is increasingly politically isolated with limited Iranian resupply capacity.

❌ Arguments Against the War

⚖️
Widely Considered Illegal

UN Special Rapporteur Ben Saul: "This is not lawful self-defence... Preventive disarmament, counterterrorism and regime change constitute the international crime of aggression." Italy called it "outside the rules of international law."

👶
Civilian Casualties Including Children

UN experts characterized the strike on a girls' school in Minab killing 160+ children as a "potential war crime." WHO identified 13 Iranian health facilities struck. Iranian Red Crescent reports 6,668 civilian units targeted.

💰
Massive Economic Disruption

Oil briefly hit $120/barrel; Strait of Hormuz effectively closed to commercial shipping; IMF chief warned of global inflation risks; G7 planning emergency meeting on strategic oil reserves.

🎯
No Clear Endgame

Analyst: "The military instrument has been authorised far beyond what the strategic objective can deliver. The US can destroy Iran's hardware, but it cannot manufacture a political alternative from the air."

⏱️
Protracted War Risk

ACLED: "Given that the Islamic Republic is more likely to continue fighting than to concede, the war's trajectory may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated."

🕊️
Hardliner Succession

Mojtaba Khamenei's appointment signals continuity of hardline policies. Top Iranian official told CNN government is "prepared for a long war" and ruled out diplomacy "for now."

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States

Trump: War will end "pretty quickly"; called it a "short-term excursion to get rid of some evil." Claims 80% of Iran's missile launchers destroyed.

🇮🇱 Israel

82% public support. Netanyahu seeks to "destabilize the regime and enable change." Army chief warns war "will take a long time."

🇪🇺 NATO/EU

NATO's Rutte: Europe is "supportive"; US "knows what it is doing." France authorized US use of bases. EU's von der Leyen reportedly supports regime change.

🇦🇺 Australia

Deploying surveillance aircraft and sending missiles to UAE; granted asylum to 5 Iranian women's soccer players. No troops on ground.

🇷🇺 Russia

Putin condemned attacks as "cynical violation of all norms of human morality and international law." Reports indicate Russia sharing targeting intelligence with Iran.

🇨🇳 China

Criticized US-Israeli actions; urged immediate end to military operations and return to diplomatic negotiations.

🇮🇹 Italy

Defense Minister: US-Israeli decision to attack Iran was "outside the rules of international law." Closing Tehran embassy.

🇪🇸 Spain

Denied US use of military bases for Iran offensive; Trump threatened economic retaliation.

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

PM Starmer: "I do not believe in regime change from the skies." UK providing defensive support and base access but not participating in strikes.

🇹🇷 Turkey

Seeking mediator role; FM Fidan pushing for ceasefire. NATO shot down 2 Iranian missiles entering Turkish airspace.

🇸🇦 Gulf States

Saudi Arabia condemned Iranian counter-strikes "in strongest terms." All Gulf states intercepting Iranian missiles/drones; Bahrain declared force majeure on energy operations.

🇺🇳 UN Experts

"Unlawful military intervention is not a solution." Called school strike a "potential war crime." Demanded immediate cessation of hostilities.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Duration of Conflict: Trump says "pretty quickly"; Israeli army chief says "long time"; Iranian officials say prepared for "long war." ACLED warns war "may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated."

Iran's Remaining Capabilities: IRGC claims it is still deploying missiles with 1+ tonne warheads; analysts suggest Iran may be rationing missiles for longer war; 400 kg of 60% enriched uranium location unknown.

Post-Conflict Governance: No coherent strategy for aftermath. Analyst: "The US can destroy Iran's hardware, but it cannot manufacture a political alternative from the air." Mojtaba Khamenei's appointment suggests hardline continuity.

Economic Impact: Oil briefly hit $120; now ~$90. IMF chief advised policymakers to prepare for "the unthinkable." G7 planning emergency meeting on strategic reserves.
