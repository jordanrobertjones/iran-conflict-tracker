📋 What Happened — Background

March 11, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched joint strikes on Iran—codenamed Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel)—targeting Iranian military infrastructure, leadership, and nuclear capabilities. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in the first wave of strikes along with dozens of senior officials.

Now in Day 12, Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has described this as the "most intense day" of strikes, with over 5,000 targets hit. Mojtaba Khamenei was appointed as Iran's new Supreme Leader on March 8. Iran has launched 37 waves of retaliatory strikes targeting Israel and US bases across the Gulf. Iran's joint military command now threatens to target "economic centres and banks" in the region.

The conflict has expanded significantly: Israel is intensifying strikes on Hezbollah in Lebanon (570+ dead, 700,000 displaced); Iranian missiles have struck multiple Gulf states including UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait and Saudi Arabia; and Turkey deployed Patriot systems after missiles entered Turkish airspace. Iran has blocked the Strait of Hormuz, causing Brent crude to spike above $110/barrel.

Total casualties: Over 1,300 civilians killed in Iran (per Iranian officials), 570 in Lebanon, 13 in Israel, and 8 US service members. 140 US troops have been wounded. An investigation is ongoing into a US strike on an Iranian girls' school that killed approximately 175 students.

✅ Arguments For the War

Degraded Iranian Military: US officials report Iran's ballistic missile launch rate has fallen 90% and drone launch rate 80-83% since Day 1. Iran's navy has been largely destroyed, and air defenses significantly weakened.
Nuclear Program Disruption: Strikes have targeted nuclear infrastructure, though recovering underground uranium stockpiles would require ground troops according to US officials.
Weakened Proxy Network: Hegseth stated Iran's proxies "Hezbollah, the Houthis and Hamas, are either broken, ineffective or on the sidelines." Israel is simultaneously striking Hezbollah infrastructure in Lebanon.
Regional Coalition: Gulf states (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain) are actively intercepting Iranian missiles and have condemned Iranian retaliation. Some sources report Qatar struck Iran after its airport was targeted.
Leadership Decapitation: The killing of Khamenei and dozens of senior IRGC commanders, including the IRGC commander and Chief of Armed Forces, has disrupted Iranian command structure.

❌ Arguments Against the War

Civilian Casualties: Over 1,300 Iranian civilians killed; nearly 10,000 civilian sites bombed. A strike on a girls' school killed ~175 students—UN experts have characterized it as a potential war crime.
International Law Violations: UN Special Rapporteur Ben Saul states the attacks are "widely considered illegal under international law" as they violate the UN Charter's prohibition on force, lack self-defense criteria, and have no UN Security Council authorization.
Expanding Regional War: Conflict has spread to Lebanon (700,000 displaced), multiple Gulf states under attack, Turkey's airspace violated. The war shows no signs of containment.
Economic Disruption: Oil prices spiked 40%+ (Brent above $110); Strait of Hormuz effectively blocked; 20% of global LNG offline; regional airports damaged or closed; massive global supply chain disruption.
Regime Change Unlikely: US National Intelligence Council assessment found "large-scale" assault unlikely to topple Iran's government. Analysts note airpower alone cannot achieve regime change.
US Costs & Casualties: Operations cost $890M-$1B per day. 8 US service members killed, 140 wounded. No congressional authorization obtained.
Hardliner Succession: Mojtaba Khamenei, a hardliner, was appointed Supreme Leader—contrary to US hopes for moderating change. Iran signals continued defiance.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States

Trump: "We've already won in many ways, but we haven't won enough." Hegseth: "Iran stands alone, and they are badly losing."

🇮🇱 Israel

IDF conducting simultaneous strikes on Iran and Hezbollah in Lebanon. Foreign Minister Sa'ar declined to rule out assassinating new Supreme Leader.

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia

Crown Prince MBS reportedly lobbied Trump to attack. Condemns Iranian strikes as aggression. Actively intercepting Iranian missiles.

🇦🇪 UAE

Intercepted 262 missiles, ~1,400 drones. Six civilian deaths. Signaled willingness to respond militarily to Iran.

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

PM Starmer: "I do not believe in regime change from the skies." UK participating in defensive roles only, not offensive strikes.

🇪🇸 Spain

PM Sánchez: War is a "big error." Denied US use of bases for offensive operations. Trump threatened trade embargo in response.

🇨🇳 China & 🇷🇺 Russia

"Robust denunciations" at UN Security Council. Putin discussing with Gulf leaders; EU President Costa: "So far, there is only one winner in this war — Russia."

🇺🇳 UN / Human Rights Bodies

UN experts: "Unprovoked attacks...launched amid diplomatic negotiations." ICRC warns of "devastating consequences for civilians." WHO identified 13 Iranian health sites hit.

🇹🇷 Turkey

NATO member caught in middle. Deployed Patriot systems after Iranian missiles entered airspace. Not allowing bases for offensive strikes; 15% of gas comes from Iran.

🇱🇧 Lebanon

President Aoun called for international help to disarm Hezbollah; seeking direct talks with Israel. 700,000 displaced; rare public backlash against Hezbollah.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

War Duration: Trump gives conflicting signals—calling it "pretty much complete" while also saying "we haven't won enough." White House says war ends when Trump "determines objectives are met." Initial 4-6 week timeline uncertain.

Ground Troops: Hegseth has not ruled out sending US ground troops. CNN reports recovering Iran's underground uranium would require significant ground forces.

Regime Change Viability: US intelligence assessment says large-scale assault unlikely to topple government. Analyst: "Nothing can guarantee regime change. We don't know how to do it."

Iranian Endurance: Iranian officials say "no room" for diplomacy and are "prepared for a long war." Vow not to allow "one liter of oil" to leave region until attacks cease.

Lebanon Front: Whether Israel-Lebanon conflict will expand or de-escalate remains unclear. Lebanon signals willingness to talk but Hezbollah continues strikes.
