📋 What Happened — Background

March 13, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated surprise airstrikes on Iran, codenamed "Operation Epic Fury" (US) and "Operation Roaring Lion" (Israel). The strikes killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior Iranian officials, marking the most significant US military action in the Middle East since the 2003 invasion of Iraq. The stated objectives include destroying Iran's nuclear and ballistic missile programs, eliminating IRGC leadership, and neutralizing Iranian naval capabilities.

The operation came after failed diplomatic negotiations in February 2026, with Omani mediators stating that "a peace deal is within reach" just days before the strikes began. Iran was arguably at its weakest point following massive protests in January 2026 that were brutally suppressed, weakened regional proxies, and economic deterioration.

Iran has responded with waves of missile and drone attacks targeting Israel, US military bases across the region, and Gulf states hosting US forces. Mojtaba Khamenei, the late Supreme Leader's son, was appointed as Iran's new Supreme Leader on March 8, 2026.

Day 14 Update (March 13): Israeli fighter jets have struck 200 additional targets in Iran, including an "extensive wave" of strikes on Tehran during Al Quds Day rallies. Iran continues retaliatory attacks across the region, with two people killed in Oman and explosions reported in Dubai. A US KC-135 refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq (not due to hostile fire), killing four crew members and bringing total US military deaths to 11. French President Macron announced one French soldier was killed in an Iranian drone strike at a base in Iraqi Kurdistan—the first French fatality of the war. The UN reports 3.2 million displaced in Iran and over 820,000 in Lebanon.

✅ Arguments For the War

⚔️Degrading Nuclear Threat: Strikes have significantly set back Iran's nuclear program and destroyed ballistic missile infrastructure that could have overwhelmed Israeli defenses. CSIS analysis notes Iran had 460 kg of 60% enriched uranium that could be weaponized in 7-10 days.
🎯Leadership Decapitation: The assassination of Supreme Leader Khamenei and dozens of senior officials created unprecedented disruption, though the regime stabilized faster than expected with Mojtaba Khamenei's appointment.
🛡️Degraded Iranian Military: US has struck over 6,000 targets; destroyed over 90 Iranian naval vessels including 30+ mine-laying ships; Iranian missile launch rate has declined fourfold since the start of the war, suggesting depleted stockpiles.
📉Weakened Proxy Network: Iran's "forward defense" doctrine is reaching its limits—Hezbollah faces isolation in Lebanon, Syrian militias marginalized after Assad's fall, Iraqi militias fractured. RAND experts note proxies are "fighting different wars."
🇮🇷Regime Vulnerability: Operation launched when Iran was at its weakest—following January 2026 protests that killed 30,000, collapsing economy, and diplomatic isolation after attacks on Gulf neighbors.

❌ Arguments Against the War

⚖️International Law Violations: The attacks are "widely considered illegal under international law" per legal experts, violating UN Charter prohibition on use of force without Security Council authorization. UN Special Rapporteur Ben Saul stated the case is "nowhere close to being self-defense against an imminent attack."
💀Civilian Casualties: At least 1,348 Iranian civilians killed, 17,000+ injured; 687 killed in Lebanon (including 98 children); 296+ children killed across the region per UNICEF. A strike on an elementary school killed 175 people including many children, linked to outdated intelligence data.
💰Global Economic Shock: Oil prices exceeded $100/barrel; Strait of Hormuz effectively closed; Goldman Sachs forecasts 20% higher oil prices for 2026; UN WFP warns of long-term global food price increases. Qatar's energy minister warned this "will bring down economies of the world."
🎯Unclear Endgame: War aims have shifted repeatedly; experts call it a "gamble" with no clear exit strategy. Brookings VP Suzanne Maloney: "What we're seeing is going to be more complicated than the White House may have hoped." Ground troops likely needed for regime change.
📊Low Public Support: Only 25% of Americans support the attacks per Reuters/IPSOS polling. Trump has not made the case to the American public for military action.
🌍Regional Destabilization: 3.2 million displaced in Iran; 820,000+ in Lebanon; Gulf states' economic model under threat; NATO ally Turkey facing energy shortages and inflation; war spreading to Iraq, Lebanon, and threatening Red Sea/Suez Canal.
⚠️US Military Costs: Operation costs ~$900 million per day; 11 US service members killed; munitions stockpiles being depleted; readiness for China/homeland defense at risk per Atlantic Council analysis.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Duration of conflict: Trump suggested 4-week timeline but Israeli Defense Minister says "no time limit." Experts warn regime change would require ground troops and could become protracted. RAND notes Iran's regime "more likely to continue fighting than to concede."

Economic fallout: Goldman Sachs forecasts oil averaging $100+/barrel in March. If Strait of Hormuz remains closed beyond 3 weeks, oil could hit $93/barrel by year-end. Analysts warn of possible global recession if disruption continues.

Regime stability: Mojtaba Khamenei's appointment "stabilized the regime faster than many observers expected." IRGC tightening grip on political system despite strikes. Whether regime can survive sustained pressure remains uncertain.

Regional escalation: Hezbollah conducting joint operations with Iran. If Yemeni Houthis and all Iraqi militias fully activate, war could expand to Red Sea and threaten Suez Canal. France preparing naval mission to reopen Hormuz.

Nuclear material: CSIS warns Iran still possesses 400 kg of 60% enriched uranium in unknown location. If regime collapses, nuclear scientists could pose proliferation risks to non-state actors.

Ground invasion: Senator Blumenthal warned Trump approach "could eventually lead to deployment of ground forces." Experts say ground troops required for stated goals, but Trump wary given 25% public approval.
