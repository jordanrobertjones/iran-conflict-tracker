📋 What Happened — Background

April 04, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched a massive, coordinated surprise military campaign against Iran — codenamed Operation Epic Fury by the U.S. and Operation Roaring Lion by Israel. President Trump announced the attacks in a Truth Social post at 2:00 AM EST. The stated objectives were to destroy Iran's ballistic missile infrastructure and nuclear program, eliminate IRGC leadership, annihilate Iran's naval capabilities, and neutralize Iranian-backed regional proxies. Regime change — though not formally declared as a goal — was widely understood as the campaign's ultimate ambition.

Just before the strikes began, Oman's Foreign Minister Badr Al-Busaidi said a "breakthrough" had been reached and Iran had agreed both to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full verification by the IAEA, adding that peace was "within reach." After the US and Israel attacked Iran, Al-Busaidi said that he was dismayed and that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined.

The initial wave of strikes — nearly 900 in 12 hours — killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of other officials, but also killed about 170 people when a missile struck a girls' school adjacent to a naval base in Minab. In the hours that followed, Iran initiated large-scale retaliation, expanding the conflict beyond Iranian territory into a region-wide exchange that touched multiple Gulf states. What began as a concentrated campaign has developed into a sustained regional war with an expanding set of targets.

Mojtaba Khamenei was elected on 8 March 2026 to replace his father as supreme leader, and the IRGC, as well as Iran's top leaders, pledged their allegiance to him. In late March the NYT described Iran's leadership as paralyzed with a severely disrupted decision-making process, and reported that damage to communications infrastructure caused paranoia and internal power struggles.

Now entering its 36th day, the conflict has spread across at least a dozen countries, closed the Strait of Hormuz — the world's major oil artery — and killed more than 2,300 people in the region. The war's economic impact has been described as the world's largest supply disruption since the 1970s energy crisis, including surges in oil and gas prices, wide disruptions in aviation and tourism, and volatility in financial markets. Trump has said if Iran doesn't reopen the strait to all traffic by April 6, he will order the destruction of Iran's energy plants.

✅ Arguments For the War

🛡️ Nuclear Program Severely Degraded

Operation Midnight Hammer in June 2025 had already decimated Iran's enrichment facilities at Fordow and Natanz and destroyed Iran's metallurgy facilities at Isfahan. Netanyahu asserted that the U.S.–Israeli strikes have eliminated Iran's ability to produce nuclear weapons and ballistic missiles, thereby removing "two existential threats" to Israel.

🚀 Iranian Missile Capability Rapidly Suppressed

By the tenth day of the war, Iranian missile and drone attacks had dropped by more than 90%, a reduction steeper than during the 2025 conflict, credited to effective suppression efforts by US and Israeli missions over Iran. US forces confirmed Iran's naval capability in the Gulf of Oman has been neutralized, with all 11 previously active warships reported sunk.

✊ Iranian Regime Decapitated and Destabilized

Israel carried out decapitation strikes on top Iranian officials and played a central role in suppressing the regime's air defense systems. In January 2026, Iranian security forces had killed thousands of protesters in their crackdown of the largest Iranian protests since the 1979 Islamic Revolution, with the regime already severely weakened entering the war.

🌍 Gulf Arab States Support Coalition

According to The Washington Post, Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia Mohammed bin Salman had multiple phone calls with Trump urging him to attack Iran. A March 2026 survey by the Israel Democracy Institute found that 82% of the Israeli public supported ongoing military operations, including 93% of Jewish Israelis.

📡 Electronic & Cyber Dominance Established

April 3 marked a significant escalation in the electronic domain as the US activated EA-37B Compass Call electronic attack aircraft to actively jam Iranian radar, navigation, and command communications across the entire theater. Initial reports indicated that Iranian internet connectivity dropped by at least 46%, suggesting that massive cyber operations are underway.

❌ Arguments Against the War

⚖️ War Launched Over Active Diplomacy — Legality Questioned

Just before strikes began, Oman's FM reported a diplomatic "breakthrough" had been reached; Iran had agreed to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full IAEA verification. Critics at the Stimson Center argue Trump initiated the war without congressional approval, without serious public debate, and in the face of public opposition, calling it "unconstitutional, unwise, and a betrayal."

🌊 Strait of Hormuz Blockade Devastates Global Economy

Iran's Hormuz blockade has sent global oil prices soaring above $100 per barrel — a jump of roughly 40% from before the war — forcing countries, particularly in Asia, to ration fuel and cut industrial production. IMF chief Kristalina Georgieva said every 10% increase in energy prices over 2026 is expected to increase global inflation by almost half a percent.

💀 Severe Civilian Casualties and Infrastructure Damage

At least 2,076 people have been killed and 26,500 wounded in Iran since the start of the war, and Iran's Ministry of Foreign Affairs says more than 600 schools and education centres have been hit. Two US strikes on the B1 bridge between Tehran and Karaj — described as the highest bridge in the Middle East — killed eight people and wounded 95, causing it to collapse; experts assessed this as a war crime.

✈️ US Aircraft Losses & Military Readiness Risk

On April 3, Iranian forces shot down a U.S. Air Force F-15E Strike Eagle over Iran with two crew members; the pilot was rescued while the search for the WSO is underway. During the rescue, an A-10 Warthog was also downed and two Black Hawks were hit. Analysts at the Atlantic Council warn the Iran war is degrading US military readiness for homeland defense and China, and will likely drain inventories of critical munitions and parts.

🌐 No Clear Endgame; Diplomacy at Zero Trust

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi said that while Iran has received messages from the US, trust remains "at zero" for any potential negotiations. Analysts agree the U.S.'s endgame is unclear, making it difficult to determine how long the military operation will take. Iran rejected the 15-point US peace plan and has asserted that Lebanon must be included as part of any ceasefire deal, making it conditional on an end to the 2026 Lebanon war.

🇨🇳 China–Russia–Iran Alignment Deepening

China's Foreign Minister Wang Yi said US and Israeli attacks on Iran were a violation of international law. The growing alignment between China, Russia, and Iran raises new concerns about the trajectory of the conflict, with analysts warning Beijing's expanding support could complicate U.S. strategy.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇮🇱 Israel

Netanyahu characterized the operation as joint and thanked Trump for his "historic leadership." On March 29, Israeli official Yuli Edelstein said the strikes had set Iran back by years, but had not yet succeeded in removing the Iranian threat entirely.

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia

Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman reportedly had multiple phone calls with Trump urging him to attack Iran. Saudi Arabia condemned in the "strongest terms" the Iranian attacks on Gulf Arab states and warned of "dire consequences."

🇨🇦 Canada

Prime Minister Mark Carney said Canada supports US efforts to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon and from further threatening international peace and security.

🇦🇷 Argentina

Argentina, under pro-Trump President Javier Milei, officially designated Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) as a "terrorist" organisation.

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

PM Starmer said he did "not believe in regime change from the skies" and the UK has not participated militarily in the strikes, though UK aircraft have been deployed in a defensive role to intercept missiles in Qatar, Jordan, Iraq, and Cyprus. The UK convened talks with 35 countries on reopening the Strait of Hormuz, focused on post-fighting measures.

🇪🇺 European Union

EU Commission President Ursula von der Leyen and Council President Antonio Costa called the conflict "greatly concerning" and urged restraint. European nations had initially refused to get involved in reopening the Strait of Hormuz, fearing they would be dragged into the war, but as oil prices spiked, EU nations decided to join the UK's coalition.

🇳🇦 NATO

NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte said Europe is "supportive" of US attacks on Iran as Iran is a "threat," and that he felt the US "knows what it is doing." A NATO spokesperson stated they were closely watching the situation while heightening missile defence vigilance to protect member states.

🇨🇳 China

China's Foreign Minister Wang Yi said US and Israeli attacks on Iran were a violation of international law. China also stated that the war should not have started, while criticising Iran for attacking other countries in the region.

🇷🇺 Russia / 🇹🇷 Turkey

Russian President Vladimir Putin and Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdogan called for an immediate ceasefire during a phone call, the Kremlin said, warning the fighting risks global impacts on energy, trade and logistics.

🇵🇰 Pakistan

Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar "strongly condemned the unwarranted attacks against Iran and called for an immediate halt to escalation through urgent resumption of diplomacy." Pakistan also said it will continue trying to push Washington and Tehran towards ending the war but admitted there are "obstacles" to peace efforts.

🇺🇳 United Nations

UN Secretary-General António Guterres warned that the Middle East conflict risks spiralling into a wider war, and called for an immediate halt to US-Israeli strikes on Iran and Iranian attacks on its neighbours. UN Human Rights Chief Volker Türk said the "reckless" war is disproportionately impacting civilians in the Middle East and beyond.

🇮🇷 Iran

Iran's military said the war will continue until its enemies face "humiliation" and "surrender," specifically cautioning the US against a ground invasion. Iran called US demands "maximalist and irrational" and denied any negotiations were under way on a ceasefire.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Strait of Hormuz deadline (April 6): Trump has said if Iran doesn't reopen the strait to all traffic by April 6, he will order the destruction of Iran's energy plants. Iran has doubled down, threatening to "completely" close the Strait of Hormuz and strike vital infrastructure across the region. French President Emmanuel Macron called the idea of using force to reopen the Strait "unrealistic," warning it would leave cargo ships vulnerable to Iranian attacks.

Ceasefire talks — disputed and murky: Trump said Iran's president asked the US for a ceasefire, with the US willing to "consider" the offer only once the Strait of Hormuz is open and clear. Tehran denied that it requested a ceasefire. Iranian officials described a recent airstrike on policy expert Kamal Kharazi — who was reportedly overseeing a possible meeting between Iranian officials and US VP JD Vance — as an attempt to derail diplomacy.

Iran's new Supreme Leader: Iran's new supreme leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, is the son of the deceased Ali Khamenei; many analysts believe he will be a continuation — and potentially more extreme version — of his father. His selection is controversial even within Iran's leadership, with reports that his appointment contravened his father's written wishes and was opposed by senior political figures.

UN Security Council vote: On April 4, 2026, the vote on Bahrain's proposal at the UN Security Council is uncertain because of divisions among major powers.

US munitions and readiness strain: CSIS analysts note that replenishing US munitions inventory after this campaign will take time, creating near-term risk. US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth abruptly fired the US Army's top general and two other senior officers, prompting speculation of a wartime leadership shake-up.
