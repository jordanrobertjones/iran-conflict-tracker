📋 What Happened — Background

March 17, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched coordinated surprise airstrikes on Iran in what the U.S. dubbed Operation Epic Fury and Israel called Operation Roaring Lion. The opening salvo killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior Iranian officials. The strikes came just one day after Oman's Foreign Minister announced a diplomatic "breakthrough" was within reach.

Now on Day 17, the conflict has killed more than 2,300 people across the region, including over 1,400 in Iran (with 200+ children), 886 in Lebanon, 15 in Israel, and 13 U.S. service members. The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed, disrupting one-fifth of global oil consumption. Iran has launched approximately 700 missiles and 3,600 drones at U.S. and Israeli targets since the war began.

Mojtaba Khamenei was elected as Iran's new Supreme Leader on March 8. U.S. intelligence assessments indicate that despite the relentless airstrikes, Iran's regime will likely remain in place—weakened but more hardline, with the IRGC exerting greater control.

✅ Arguments For the War

✓
Degradation of Iranian Military Capability

The U.S. has destroyed 17 Iranian warships including a submarine, while more than 50 naval vessels are reportedly on the sea floor. Iranian ballistic missile launch rates have declined significantly from the war's opening days, indicating depleted capabilities.

✓
Nuclear Infrastructure Damage

The Natanz nuclear facility has sustained damage and Fordow remains inoperable. Defense industrial facilities needed to reconstitute enrichment capacity are being systematically targeted. However, a leaked DIA report suggests much of Iran's enriched uranium stockpile may have been moved before strikes.

✓
Regime Leadership Eliminated

The opening strikes killed Supreme Leader Khamenei and dozens of senior officials. Abu Ali al-Askari, security commander of Kata'ib Hezbollah, was killed March 14. The head of Basij forces and over a dozen senior Basij figures were targeted in joint U.S.-Israeli strikes.

✓
Air Superiority Achieved

U.S. and Israeli forces established effective control of airspace from western Iran to central Tehran within 24 hours. CENTCOM states the "two most powerful air forces in the world are dominating the skies over the world's largest state sponsor of terror."

❌ Arguments Against the War

✗
Massive Civilian Casualties

More than 1,400 Iranian civilians have been killed including over 200 children. The WHO identified 13 Iranian health infrastructure sites hit during the war. Critics argue this creates a moral burden the U.S. and Israel will carry.

✗
Global Economic Disruption

The Strait of Hormuz closure has halted roughly 14.7 million barrels of crude and 4.8 million barrels of refined products that moved daily—one-fifth of global oil consumption. The war costs the U.S. approximately $890 million per day, with total costs estimated at $16.5 billion through 12 days. The UN World Food Programme warns of long-term increases in global food prices.

✗
Regional Destabilization

Iran has attacked Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Jordan, Oman, Iraq, Turkey, Azerbaijan, and Cyprus. Gulf cities including Dubai and Doha face economic stability risks. Dubai International Airport was damaged by drone strikes. Approximately 200 U.S. troops have been injured in seven countries.

✗
Diplomatic Failure

Oman's Foreign Minister announced a diplomatic "breakthrough" one day before strikes began. Critics argue the U.S. undermined "active and serious negotiations" that had made "significant progress, including addressing Israel's security concerns."

✗
Strategic Overextension Risk

Critics like Fareed Zakaria argue the U.S. is falling into an "imperial trap," noting: "Great powers do not usually fall because they are conquered by foreign armies. They fall because they overextend themselves on the periphery while neglecting the core."

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States

Trump claims Iran's military has been "decimated" but says he's "still not declaring it over." Seeking international coalition to secure Strait of Hormuz with limited success.

🇮🇱 Israel

IDF says it has "thousands of targets" remaining in Iran. Finance Minister warned Lebanon's Dahiyeh area would "look like Khan Younis."

🇬🇧 NATO/UK

NATO Secretary General Rutte says Europe is "supportive" of U.S. attacks, calling Iran a "threat." UK's Akrotiri base in Cyprus was targeted by Iranian drones.

🇷🇺 Russia

Putin called Khamenei's assassination a "cynical violation of all norms." Russia is reportedly providing Iran with intelligence on U.S. warship and aircraft positions. Abstained from UN resolution condemning Iranian attacks on Gulf states.

🇨🇳 China

Foreign Minister Wang Yi stated "China opposes any military strikes launched by Israel and the US against Iran" and called for immediate cessation of military operations. Called it "a war that should not have happened."

🇪🇸 Spain

PM Sánchez called the war a "big error," withdrew ambassador to Israel permanently on March 11. Trump threatened trade halt and embargo.

🇫🇷 France

Macron said France "cannot approve of" strikes conducted "outside of international law," while noting Iran bears primary responsibility.

🇳🇴 Norway

Foreign Minister stated the preventive strike is "not in line with international law" because it requires "an immediately imminent threat."

🇪🇺 EU

Foreign ministers decided against expanding naval operations around the Strait of Hormuz despite Trump's appeals. Leaders called conflict "greatly concerning" and urged restraint.

🇨🇭 Switzerland

Rejected U.S. military reconnaissance flight requests, citing neutrality law, while allowing non-combat transport flights.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Will regime change occur? U.S. intelligence assessments say Iran's regime will likely remain in place, "weakened but more hard-line, with the IRGC exerting greater control." Some Iranian opposition mood has shifted as civilian casualties mount.

How will war end? Iran's FM says Tehran seeks neither "truce nor talks" until it receives guarantees against future attacks and reparations. Trump says the war will continue until Iran has "no capacity whatsoever" to develop weapons for "a very long period of time."

Nuclear stockpile status? A leaked DIA report assessed Iran may have moved much of its enriched uranium before strikes, setting back weapons capability by only months. CIA Director Ratcliffe countered that new information shows "severe damage" requiring years to rebuild.

Russia's intelligence role? U.S. officials report Russia is providing Iran with real-time data on American warship and aircraft positions. Defense Secretary Hegseth responded only: "We're tracking everything."
