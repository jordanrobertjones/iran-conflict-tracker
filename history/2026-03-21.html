📋 What Happened — Background

March 21, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel), a coordinated surprise attack against Iran. The stated objectives were to dismantle Iran's nuclear and ballistic missile programs, induce regime change, and eliminate IRGC leadership. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in the initial strikes, and his son Mojtaba Khamenei was selected as successor. The attack came just one day after Oman's Foreign Minister announced a diplomatic "breakthrough" was "within reach."

As of Day 21 (March 20), the conflict has killed over 1,400 Iranians and injured more than 18,000 civilians. At least 18 Israelis, 13 US service members, and dozens across Gulf states have been killed. US CENTCOM reports striking over 7,000 targets in Iran. The war has spread to multiple countries, with Iran retaliating against US bases and Gulf states. Israel has expanded its ground operation into Lebanon, where over 1,000 have died and a fifth of the population has been displaced.

The South Pars gasfield strike has escalated energy tensions. Netanyahu agreed to halt Israeli strikes on Iranian energy sites after Trump reacted angrily, exposing cracks in US-Israel coordination. Iran has warned of "zero restraint" if energy facilities are attacked again. The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed to Western shipping, causing the largest oil supply disruption in history.

✅ Arguments For the War

⚔️Iranian ballistic missile launches have dropped by more than 90% (from 350 on Feb 28 to ~25 by March 14), with Israeli intelligence estimating over 70% of Iran's missile launchers have been neutralized
🎯Iran's air defenses suppressed to the point that US B-1 bombers are flying over Iranian airspace—a signal of near-total air dominance
🔬Significant damage to Iran's nuclear facilities at Natanz and Fordow, though IAEA Director Grossi warns "the material will still be there and the enrichment capacities will be there"
🚢17 Iranian warships destroyed including a submarine; naval capabilities for closing Hormuz significantly degraded
🏛️Major decapitation of Iranian leadership: Supreme Leader Khamenei killed, along with defense minister Nasirzadeh, IRGC commanders, and National Security Council head Ali Larijani
📉Iran's "Axis of Resistance" weakened: Hezbollah fighting for survival in Lebanon; Iraqi militias degraded; Houthis remaining on sidelines so far

❌ Arguments Against the War

⛽Largest supply shock in oil market history: crude prices up ~45% ($110+/barrel), with IEA urging work-from-home measures to ease global fuel crisis
🌊Strait of Hormuz effectively closed—one-fifth of global oil supply cut off; 20,000 seafarers stranded in the Gulf
🏥Severe humanitarian toll: 18 hospitals hit in Iran; 204 children killed including the Minab school strike (170+ schoolgirls dead, described as potential war crime by UN experts)
💰Trump may seek $200 billion in new Pentagon funding; war described as morphing from "war of choice" to "war of necessity" with no clear exit
🇱🇧Lebanon crisis: 1,000+ dead, 20% of population displaced in just two weeks; Al-Aqsa mosque closed during Eid for first time since 1967
⚠️Houthis threatening potential Bab al-Mandab blockade—would force ships around Africa, compounding the Hormuz closure
☢️Iran's 400kg of 60% enriched uranium location remains unknown; nuclear scientists could scatter, creating new proliferation risks

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States: Trump considering "winding down" war while simultaneously sending USS Boxer with Marines to Persian Gulf. Administration lifting sanctions on Iranian oil at sea until April 19 to ease prices.
🇮🇱 Israel: 82% public support (93% among Jewish Israelis). Netanyahu hints at potential ground operations in Iran. Agreed to halt energy site strikes after US pressure.
🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia: Crown Prince MBS vowed military force against Iranian incursions. Intercepting 64+ drones daily. Rerouting oil through East-West pipeline to Yanbu.
🇦🇪 UAE: Absorbed bulk of Iranian attacks (1,440 drones, 253 ballistic missiles). 90% interception rate. Receiving expedited US weapons sales.
🇬🇧 United Kingdom: PM Starmer: "does not believe in regime change from the skies." UK not participating militarily but warned Iran against targeting UK bases.
🇫🇷 France: Macron says strikes conducted "outside of international law." Condemned UNIFIL peacekeeper attack in Lebanon.
🇩🇪 Germany: Chancellor Merz acknowledges "dilemma" over legality. Germany has "no intention" of joining war; EU has "no appetite" to send troops.
🇳🇴 Norway: Foreign Minister Eide: "Preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat"—strike "not in line with international law."
🇪🇺 European Union: Urgently calling for moratorium on strikes against energy and water facilities. EC warned about settler violence in West Bank.
🇴🇲 Oman: Foreign Minister Al-Busaidi "dismayed" that "active and serious negotiations" were undermined by strikes one day after announcing breakthrough.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Houthi wildcard: Despite weeks of bellicose rhetoric, Houthis have not attacked Red Sea shipping. On March 20, senior official al-Bukhaiti announced the group is "considering" a Bab al-Mandab blockade targeting "aggressor countries." Analysts cite depleted weapons stockpiles, fear of Israeli retaliation, and the 2025 US ceasefire deal as restraining factors—but intervention could come "at any moment."

Iran's endgame unclear: New Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei reportedly injured and in hiding, but Professor Kamrava notes the system "continues to function" through decentralized IRGC command. Iran says it will continue fighting even if the US stops. Expert Karim Sadjadpour: "I don't think that President Trump is going to simply be able to end the war and claim victory."

Nuclear concerns persist: IAEA's Grossi warns Iran's enrichment capacity and nuclear material "will still be there" after fighting ends. Rep. Bill Foster raised concern that the administration "never had a plan for that nuclear stockpile."
