📋 What Happened — Background

April 07, 2026

On February 27, 2026, President Trump ordered Operation Epic Fury while aboard Air Force One. At 3:38 p.m. EST, Trump gave the order to proceed, and U.S. missiles, drones, and Israeli fighter jets began striking Iran the next day around 9:45 a.m. IRST. The operation was codenamed Operation Roaring Lion by Israel. The outset of the conflict was communicated unconventionally: President Trump announced the attacks in a TruthSocial post at 2:00 AM EST, with no address to the media or public briefing to Congress beyond a notification to the Gang of Eight shortly before strikes commenced.

Just before the strikes began, Omani foreign minister Badr Al-Busaidi said a "breakthrough" had been reached and Iran had agreed both to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full verification by the IAEA, saying peace was "within reach." After the US and Israel attacked Iran, Al-Busaidi said that he was dismayed and that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined.

On February 28, Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was assassinated in an Israeli air attack on his compound; early on March 1, Iranian state media confirmed his death. Iranian defense minister Aziz Nasirzadeh and IRGC commander Mohammad Pakpour were also killed by Israeli airstrikes, along with Defence Council secretary Ali Shamkhani and four top Ministry of Intelligence officials — seven Iranian security leaders in all confirmed dead. Mojtaba Khamenei was elected on March 8, 2026, to replace his father as supreme leader, with Iran's top leaders pledging allegiance to him.

The Israeli Air Force said it struck 500 military targets in western and central Iran in the largest combat sortie in its history, also targeting Iranian naval vessels, using over 1,200 bombs in the first 24 hours. The opening strikes inflicted over a hundred civilian casualties; Iran responded with missile and drone strikes against Israel, U.S. bases, and U.S.-allied countries in the Middle East, and by closing the Strait of Hormuz, disrupting global trade.

Now in its 38th day (as of April 7, 2026), the conflict has expanded dramatically. Iran rejected a U.S. plan for a temporary ceasefire and countered with a proposal for a permanent end to the war, as President Trump threatened to bomb Iranian bridges and power plants if Iran did not open the Strait of Hormuz. Trump set a specific deadline of 8pm ET (midnight GMT) on Tuesday, April 7. Israel has approved an updated target list of energy and infrastructure sites in Iran in preparation for a contingency scenario in which U.S. diplomatic talks fail; the Israelis are highly skeptical that a deal is achievable.

At least 2,076 people have been killed by U.S.-Israeli attacks on Iran since February 28, according to Iran's Health Ministry, with over 26,500 injured, including at least 4,000 women and 1,621 children. The U.S. military has confirmed 13 fatalities from Iranian attacks across the region. The conflict between Hezbollah and Israel has escalated into a 2026 Lebanon war, killing more than 1,400 people, both militants and civilians, by early April.

✅ Arguments For the War

PRONeutralizing Iran's Military Command Structure: Israel carried out decapitation strikes on top Iranian officials and played a central role in suppressing the regime's air defense systems. U.S. forces confirmed Iran's naval capability in the Gulf of Oman has been neutralized, with all 11 previously active warships reported sunk.
PRODegrading Iran's Nuclear Infrastructure: Operation Midnight Hammer in June 2025 had already dealt a significant blow, decimating Iran's enrichment facilities at Fordow and Natanz and destroying metallurgy facilities at Isfahan. The Atomic Energy Organisation of Iran confirmed a U.S.-Israeli attack rendered its Khondab heavy water production plant inoperable.
PRODismantling Iranian Missile Capacity: Analysis by JINSA showed a sharp reduction in Iranian missile attacks after the first day of the war, attributing it to joint U.S.-Israeli success in targeting launchers and eliminating as many as three-quarters of Iranian missile launchers. Israel struck the South Pars petrochemical complex, with the Jam and Damavand facilities — accounting for roughly 85% of Iran's petrochemical exports — rendered inoperative, described as a "severe economic blow" costing Iran "tens of billions of dollars."
PROWeakening Iran's Regional Proxy Network: The stated objectives included eliminating IRGC leadership, annihilating Iran's naval capabilities, and neutralizing Iranian-backed regional proxies. Iran's ability to fund and sustain Hezbollah and other proxies is being severely tested as its economy buckles.
PROPotential for Iranian Political Change: The attacks came when the Iranian regime was arguably at its weakest point for years, following extensive protests in early 2026 motivated by a weakened economy and struggling infrastructure — protests put down with extensive use of force.

❌ Arguments Against the War

CONDiplomacy Sabotaged at a Critical Moment: Just before strikes began, Oman's foreign minister reported Iran had agreed to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full IAEA verification — peace was "within reach" and talks were to resume March 2. After the attack, Al-Busaidi said "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined.
CONNuclear Threat May Have Worsened: On April 2, Trump stated he does not "care" about Iran's stock of highly enriched uranium as it is underground — contradicting a key rationale for the war. Iran's HEU stock is now assessed as a more risky scenario than before the war began, leaving Iran significantly closer to nuclear capability than the Geneva settlement would have.
CONMassive Civilian Casualties and Humanitarian Crisis: Iran reports damage to at least 120 historical sites. BBC Verify analysis suggests a deadly attack on residential buildings and a sports hall in Lamerd, killing 21 people including 4 children, was carried out using a U.S. Precision Strike Missile (PrSM). According to UN Human Rights Chief Volker Türk, the "reckless" war is disproportionately impacting civilians in the Middle East and beyond.
CONUnprecedented Regional Economic Disruption: The war's economic impact has been described as the world's largest supply disruption since the 1970s energy crisis, with surges in oil and gas prices, wide disruptions in aviation and tourism, and volatility in financial markets. QatarEnergy has said Iranian strikes on a large gas field will take three to five years to repair.
CONTrump's Threats to Civilian Infrastructure Alarm Legal Experts: Trump threatened to bomb Iranian bridges and power plants; legal experts say attacking civilian infrastructure that doesn't contribute to military action would be a war crime under international and U.S. laws. Asked if he was concerned about committing a war crime, Trump said, "No."
CONNo Clear Endgame or Congressional Authorization: President Trump initiated the war without congressional approval, without a serious public debate, and in the face of overwhelming public opposition. Analysts agree that the U.S. game plan — or rather, its endgame — is not clear, making it difficult to determine how long the military operation will take.
CONIran's Strait of Hormuz Leverage Persists Post-War: Iran would not need to continue attacks indefinitely to retain the ability to deter and coerce the United States; it now has a proven ability to disrupt the global economy by shutting down the Strait, and could threaten to disrupt shipping in the future for any reason and at any time.
CONRisk of Driving Iran Toward Nuclear Weapons: A fundamental question for analysts is: can you bomb a country out of its desire for a nuclear deterrent, or does such a strike make the acquisition of an eventual nuclear weapon deterrent an existential imperative? While Epic Fury is likely to have reduced the immediate short-term risks of Iranian proliferation, the program still exists — Iran still possesses 400 kilograms of 60% enriched uranium, and the exact location of that material remains unknown.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States
Trump and Netanyahu announced the operations as reflecting close coordination, both vowing to prevent Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons and calling for the fall of the regime, though without committing to topple it directly. On April 2, Trump said the strikes have "dramatically curtailed" Iranian missile and drone systems and announced the attacks would escalate over the next two to three weeks.
🇮🇱 Israel
Netanyahu characterized it as a joint operation and thanked Trump for his "historic leadership." Netanyahu asserted that the U.S.-Israeli strikes have eliminated Iran's ability to produce nuclear weapons and ballistic missiles, thereby removing "two existential threats" to Israel.
🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia
Saudi Arabia and the UAE share the U.S. and Israel's stated objectives of restricting Iran's nuclear, drone, and missile programs. According to the Washington Post, Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman urged Trump to attack Iran in multiple phone calls, lobbying him repeatedly in the weeks leading up to the initial strikes.
🇨🇦 Canada
Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney said Canada stands with the Iranian people, "reaffirms Israel's right to defend itself," and supports the U.S. "acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon."
🇬🇧 United Kingdom
In a joint statement with France and Germany, PM Keir Starmer condemned Iranian counter-strikes and called for a resumption of diplomacy, but said he did "not believe in regime change from the skies." The UK has not participated militarily in the strikes, but has deployed aircraft in a defensive role to intercept missiles in Qatar, Jordan, Iraq, and Cyprus.
🇦🇪 UAE
UAE presidential adviser Anwar Gargash said any settlement of the war must guarantee access through the Strait of Hormuz, warning that a deal that fails to rein in Iran's nuclear programme and its missiles and drones would pave the way for "a more dangerous, more volatile Middle East." Gargash told NPR his country wants to see an end to the conflict but one that does not create "continuous instability in the region," saying "we don't want to see more and more escalation."
🇵🇰 🇹🇷 🇪🇬 Mediators
Pakistan, Egypt, and Turkey are coordinating efforts toward a negotiated settlement to end the war. Iran's state news agency IRNA said Iran rejected a U.S. proposal for a temporary ceasefire, conveying to Pakistan the need for a permanent end to the war, with demands including lifting sanctions and ending other wars in the region.
🇳🇴 Norway
Foreign Minister Espen Barth stated the initial attack on Iran by Israel breached standards of international law, saying "it is not in line with international law" as "preventive attacks require an immediately imminent threat."
🇮🇪 Ireland
Ireland's PM Michael Martin strongly urged all parties to "exercise restraint" and warned that "the use of force by the United States and Israel against Iran, and the subsequent retaliation by Iran across the region, undermine international peace and security."
🌐 United Nations
UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres and several uninvolved countries condemned the U.S.-Israeli strikes; the United Nations Security Council later passed a resolution condemning Iran's retaliatory strikes on the Gulf states. UN Human Rights Chief Volker Türk stated the "reckless" war is disproportionately impacting civilians in the Middle East and beyond.
⚖️ International Legal Community
Over 100 international law experts issued a joint warning that U.S. strikes on Iran violate the UN Charter and may constitute war crimes (April 2, 2026). Critics of the war, including legal and international relations experts, have described the attacks as illegal under U.S. law, an act of imperialism, and a violation of Iran's sovereignty under international law.
✝️ Pope Leo XIV
Pope Leo XIV rebuked those who wage war and have "hands full of blood," stating that God "does not listen to the prayers of those who wage war, but rejects them" — a response to Secretary Hegseth's prayer for "overwhelming violence of action."

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Key uncertainties as of April 7, 2026 include: Will Trump's April 7 deadline trigger infrastructure strikes? Trump set a specific deadline of 8pm ET (midnight GMT) on Tuesday, April 7 for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz or face strikes on power plants and bridges. Can diplomacy succeed? Iran has rejected proposals for a temporary ceasefire, saying it would allow adversaries to pause and prepare, instead submitting a 10-clause counter-proposal demanding ending regional hostilities, a protocol for safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz, reconstruction, and the lifting of sanctions. Will Iran's HEU stockpile be seized or destroyed? Trump admitted on April 2 he no longer cares about Iran's buried highly enriched uranium — raising fears its stockpile is now a greater nuclear proliferation risk than before the war began. Will Iran's new leadership consolidate power? In late March, the NYT described Iran's leadership as paralyzed, with severely disrupted decision-making and paranoia from damaged communications infrastructure causing internal power struggles. Will the Houthis and Hezbollah fully enter the war? The Houthis have entered the war; whether they choose to block the Bab al-Mandab strait could have significant consequences for the global economy. Long-term nuclear implications remain deeply uncertain: Analysts continue to debate whether you can bomb a country out of its desire for a nuclear deterrent, or whether such a strike makes acquisition of an eventual nuclear weapon an existential imperative for Iran.
