📋 What Happened — Background

March 16, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury and Operation Roaring Lion—a joint military campaign against Iran. The initial strikes killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and dozens of senior officials. By Day 16, the US has struck over 6,000 targets, while Israel has conducted hundreds of additional strikes. Iran has responded with approximately 700 missiles and 3,600 drones targeting Israel, US bases in the Gulf, and neighboring countries.

Mojtaba Khamenei, son of the slain leader, was appointed as the new Supreme Leader on March 8. On March 14-15, strikes on Kharg Island—which handles 90% of Iran's oil exports—escalated tensions further. Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi denied on March 15 that Iran has asked for a ceasefire, stating "We are ready to defend ourselves as long as it takes." Trump administration officials expect the conflict to end within "weeks or sooner," while the Israeli military says it has "thousands of targets" requiring at least three more weeks.

The conflict has spread to Lebanon, where Hezbollah entered the war on March 2, prompting the Lebanese government to declare Hezbollah's military wing illegal. At least 773 people have been killed in Lebanon since March 2. The war has caused major disruptions to the Strait of Hormuz and global energy markets, with oil prices surpassing $105/barrel—the highest since July 2022. An estimated 3.2 million Iranians have been displaced.

✅ Arguments For the War

✓
Degradation of Iran's Military Capabilities

Defense Secretary Hegseth stated that Iran's drone assaults are "down 95%" and missile volume is "down 90%." More than 50 Iranian naval vessels have reportedly been destroyed. Atlantic Council experts assess the US "is well on its way to achieving" its stated military objectives.

✓
Iran's Regime Weakened

Key Iranian leaders including the Supreme Leader, Defense Minister Aziz Nasirzadeh, and IRGC commander Mohammad Pakpour were killed. RAND experts note the Islamic Republic "will be fundamentally altered" even if it survives. The regime faced major internal protests in January 2026 before the conflict began.

✓
Israeli Public Support

A March 2026 survey by the Israel Democracy Institute found that 82% of the Israeli public supports ongoing military operations against Iran.

✓
Proxy Network Under Pressure

Lebanese government declared Hezbollah's military wing illegal. RAND analysts note Iran's "forward defense" doctrine is "reaching its limits" as its primary proxies are "fighting for their survival."

❌ Arguments Against the War

✗
Civilian Casualties and Humanitarian Impact

Iran's UN representative reports at least 1,348 civilians killed. A strike on a girls' elementary school in Minab killed approximately 170 people. The UN reports 3.2 million Iranians displaced. Over 56 museums, historic buildings and UNESCO World Heritage Sites have reportedly been damaged.

✗
Global Economic Disruption

Oil prices have surged above $105/barrel, US gas prices up 24% since the war began. The Strait of Hormuz closure has disrupted 20% of global oil supplies. Qatar declared Force Majeure on gas contracts. Analysts warn of potential global recession if disruptions persist.

✗
US Casualties and Costs

At least 13 US service members killed (7 in Iranian attacks, 6 in a refueling aircraft crash). Approximately 200 wounded, with 10 listed as seriously wounded. CSIS estimates Operation Epic Fury costs ~$900 million per day and has totaled $16.5 billion over 12 days.

✗
Regional Destabilization

Gulf states (UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain) have suffered Iranian retaliatory strikes. At least 773 killed in Lebanon since March 2. Iranian missile entered Turkish airspace, raising concerns about NATO Article 4 invocation. Spain denied US use of military bases, calling the war a "big error."

✗
Unclear Endgame and Regime Resilience

ACLED analysts warn the war "may prove more protracted and unpredictable than decision-makers in Washington anticipated." Time Magazine notes the Iranian regime "is unlikely to collapse" and regime supporters may "rally around the flag." Analysts question whether the goal of regime change through airstrikes is achievable.

✗
American Public Opposition

Polling shows 54% of Americans disapprove of Trump's handling of Iran; 60% say Trump has no clear plan. A Marist poll found 55% view Iran as a "minor threat or no threat at all."

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States

Trump: "We will finish this on 'America first' conditions." States he is not ready for a deal "because the terms aren't good enough yet." Called for international naval coalition for Strait of Hormuz.

🇮🇱 Israel

82% public support for operations. IDF preparing for "at least three more weeks" with "thousands of targets" remaining. Goal is to "weaken the Iranian regime severely."

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

PM Starmer said he does "not believe in regime change from the skies." UK not participating militarily but allows use of bases for "defensive" strikes. Joint statement with France/Germany condemned Iranian counter-strikes.

🇦🇷 Argentina

President Milei called Khamenei "one of the most evil, violent, and cruel individuals that human history has ever seen." Most vocal supporter in Latin America.

🇨🇦 Canada

PM Carney: "Canada supports the United States acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon." Not militarily engaged but maintains regional presence.

🇪🇺 European Union

Called conflict "greatly concerning" and urged restraint. Supports "credible transition" of power in Iran. Criticized Israeli actions in West Bank on March 10.

🇯🇵 Japan

PM Takaichi: Iran's nuclear weapons "can never be tolerated." Urged Iran to cease hostilities but made no statements on US-Israeli actions.

🇷🇺 Russia

FM Lavrov condemned strikes as "reckless" and "unprovoked act of armed aggression." Putin called for "immediate cessation of hostilities" while maintaining contact with Gulf states.

🇪🇸 Spain

Denied US use of military bases. PM Sánchez called war a "big error" dragging Europe into instability. Trump threatened trade embargo in response.

🇮🇩 Indonesia

President Prabowo offered to travel to Tehran to mediate. Paused engagement with US-initiated Board of Peace amid domestic calls for withdrawal.

🇨🇴 Colombia

Outright condemned the US and Israeli airstrikes.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Will regime change occur? Analysts are skeptical that air strikes alone can topple the regime. RAND notes the Islamic Republic is "deeply institutionalized," while Time notes the IRGC has shown resilience and Iranians may "rally around the flag."

How will conflict end? Trump administration says "weeks or sooner," Israel says 3+ more weeks of strikes needed. Iran's FM denies any ceasefire request. Atlantic Council experts note potential "daylight" between US and Israeli goals.

Nuclear stockpile status? Rep. Bill Foster raised concern that administration "never had a plan for that nuclear stockpile of enriched uranium—to destroy it, to seize it, or to put it under international inspection."

Long-term regional stability? Gulf states face economic model challenge as war threatens their positioning as safe investment destinations. Analysts warn of potential nuclear breakout scenario if Iran decides "the war provides an opportunity."
