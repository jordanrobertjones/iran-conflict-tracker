📋 What Happened — Background

April 02, 2026

On February 28, 2026, the United States Central Command (CENTCOM) and Israel launched a massive, coordinated preemptive military campaign against Iran — codenamed Operation Epic Fury by the U.S. and Operation Roaring Lion by Israel. The operation marked the most significant U.S. military action in the Middle East since the Iraq War, executed without a formal address to Congress beyond a War Powers notification and a Gang of Eight briefing. President Trump announced the strikes via an eight-minute video on Truth Social at 2:00 AM EST. The stated objectives were to destroy Iran's ballistic missile infrastructure and nuclear program, eliminate IRGC leadership, annihilate Iran's naval capabilities, and neutralize Iranian-backed regional proxies.

Beginning in late December 2025, massive nationwide anti-government protests erupted in Iran, originally driven by discontent over the economic crisis, escalating into explicit calls for the overthrow of the Islamic Republic. The protests became the largest in scale since the 1979 revolution with 5 million Iranians protesting. The Iranian government responded with massacres of protesters, with the deadliest incidents occurring on 8–10 January 2026. In mid-February, the US and Iran began a new round of indirect nuclear negotiations. The surprise US-Israeli attack was launched during those nuclear negotiations. The International Atomic Energy Agency said that while Iran has an "ambitious" nuclear program and has refused to allow inspections of its damaged sites since the 2025 war, there was no evidence of a structured nuclear weapons program when the 2026 war began.

The opening phase focused on decapitating senior Iranian leadership while degrading missile infrastructure, launch systems, and air defenses. In the hours that followed, Iran initiated large-scale retaliation, expanding the conflict beyond Iranian territory into a region-wide exchange that touched multiple Gulf states and allied military assets. On 28 February 2026, Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was assassinated in an Israeli air attack on his compound. Early on 1 March, Iranian state media announced that Khamenei had been killed. In the largest B-2 operational strike in U.S. history, the United States dropped 14 GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrators on Iran's underground nuclear facilities at Fordow and Natanz.

The conflict has now entered its 35th day (April 2, 2026). More than 9,000 targets have been struck since the launch of Operation Epic Fury, according to a March 23, 2026 fact sheet from U.S. Central Command, alongside more than 9,000 combat flights, hitting missile sites, air defenses, IRGC command centers and weapons production facilities. At least 1,937 people have been killed by US-Israeli attacks on Iran since February 28, Iran's Health Ministry says, with over 24,800 injured, including some 4,000 women and 1,621 children. The US military has confirmed 13 fatalities from Iranian attacks across the region. The number of people killed in Lebanon since Israel renewed widespread attacks now stands at 1,268, according to Lebanon's Health Ministry.

Latest developments (April 1–2, 2026): President Trump delivered his first prime-time address to the nation on the Iran war, telling Americans the war is "nearing completion" and projected another two or three weeks of involvement. Trump signaled that the US is prepared to intensify its military response and threatened to bring Iran "back to the stone ages." The two sides have been exchanging messages via intermediaries in third-party countries including Pakistan, but no face-to-face talks have been arranged. Trump said any ceasefire would be considered "when Hormuz Strait is open, free, and clear." On April 1, former Iranian foreign minister Kamal Kharazi was seriously injured and his wife was killed in an airstrike on his home. Iranian officials described the attack as an attempt to derail diplomacy, noting that Kharazi was overseeing engagement with Pakistan for a possible meeting between Iranian officials and U.S. Vice President JD Vance. Iran's Foreign Ministry spokesperson stated Tehran will continue to fight back for as long as US-Israeli strikes continue, adding that Tehran "will not tolerate this vicious cycle of war, negotiations, ceasefire." A critical deadline looms: Trump set April 6 as the cutoff for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz or face strikes on Iranian power plants.

✅ Arguments For the War

PRONuclear threat neutralized (claimed): Netanyahu asserted that the U.S.–Israeli strikes have eliminated Iran's ability to produce nuclear weapons and ballistic missiles, thereby removing "two existential threats" to Israel. U.S. and Israeli airstrikes have neutralized over 70 percent of Iran's missile launchers and driven missile production to zero, according to Israeli intelligence estimates.
PROIranian military capability severely degraded: Iran's air force, navy, air defenses, and missile production are now largely crippled. Air superiority remains firmly with Israel and the US. US forces confirmed Iran's naval capability in the Gulf of Oman has been neutralized, with all previously active warships reported sunk.
PROProxy network weakened: The war continues to weaken and fragment Iran's proxy network, degrading its capacity to execute coherent operations on behalf of Tehran — a continuation and escalation of the multi-year campaign to degrade these proxy groups. More than 400 Hezbollah fighters have been killed since the new war against Israel began on March 2.
PROIran's leadership decapitated: The campaign has decapitated much of Iran's top military and IRGC leadership, including Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei (initial strikes, February 28 – March 1), IRGC Commander-in-Chief Major General Hossein Salami, IRGC Aerospace Force Commander Amir Ali Hajizadeh, and IRGC Quds Force Commander.
PRODiplomatic off-ramp emerging: Trump's fifteen-point peace proposal suggests that he recognizes the need to find an off-ramp. Iran's actual president, Masoud Pezeshkian, said Iran is prepared to accept a ceasefire, but only if it receives security guarantees. Messages are being exchanged through Pakistan and other intermediaries.
PROGulf states joint condemnation of Iran: On 26 March 2026, the UAE along with five Gulf countries — Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, and Jordan — issued a joint condemnation about Iran and its affiliated armed groups and their attacks against countries in the region.

❌ Arguments Against the War

CONMassive civilian toll and war crimes allegations: More than 2,000 Iranians have been killed and thousands of civilian sites, including hospitals, schools, universities and pharmaceutical factories, have been attacked by Israel and the US. Targeting of civilian sites is considered a war crime. An Iranian official says US-Israeli forces bombed the Tofigh Daru pharmaceutical raw material units in Tehran, dealing a "blow to the national medical supply chain."
CONStrait of Hormuz choke-point — global economic crisis: The Strait of Hormuz has been effectively closed for nearly five weeks, throwing global oil markets into chaos with no clear end in sight. Iran's threats and attacks on vessels have stopped almost all traffic through the waterway, the main conduit for about 20% of the world's oil and natural gas. Oil prices have gone over $114 per barrel, the highest since the COVID-19 pandemic, with an expert warning this could trigger a worldwide economic recession.
CONIran may now be more determined to go nuclear: Iran's nuclear program may have been delayed, but the attacks may mean that the Iranian regime now sees gaining a nuclear weapon as integral to guaranteeing its own future survival. As CSIS commentators noted, "Iran still possesses 400 kilograms of 60 percent enriched uranium, and the exact location of that nuclear material remains unknown."
CONNo Congressional authorization — constitutional concerns: Trump's decision to go it alone in Iran without a vote or even debate in Congress creates both constitutional problems and political challenges. The president chose not to offer a full discussion and provided only a cursory public justification once the bombs started dropping.
CONProliferation incentives for other states: This use of force sends a message that regimes may be safer if they develop a nuclear program first to avoid being susceptible to US-led overthrow, then use their nuclear program to force negotiations.
CONRegime change theory historically flawed — no exit strategy: What strategic bombing campaigns have reliably produced across a century of evidence is not rebellion but solidarity. Even when populations despise their leaders, they have a powerful tendency, when bombs fall, to close ranks against the external aggressor. Iran in 2026 is likely to emerge battered but not broken. Analysts noted Trump failed to address ground troops or diplomacy. "There was almost no mention of ongoing indirect negotiations, no mention of mediators — the Pakistanis, the Turks, the Egyptians — no sense that there would be a diplomatic off-ramp," one analyst said.
CONNATO fracturing — allies withdrawing support: Spain, France and Italy have restricted US military operations, closing airspace, denying base access and limiting logistical support. Trump said in a new interview he would consider having the US leave NATO, criticizing the alliance for what he said was lackluster military support for the war.
CONCyber warfare spillover — global civilian infrastructure at risk: On March 11, 2026, employees at Stryker offices across 79 countries found blank screens displaying the logo of the Iran-linked hacking group Handala, which wiped its entire endpoint environment across six continents in three hours. In March 2026, the Islamic Cyber Resistance announced a recruitment campaign for what it described as "the great epic battle," with over 60 individual groups mobilized.

🌍 How the World Has Responded

🇺🇸 United States
Strongly Supportive

President Trump stated the objective is to "defend the American people by eliminating imminent threats from the Iranian regime" and to destroy Iran's remaining nuclear and missile capabilities. In his first prime-time address to the nation, Trump declared the war "nearing completion" and projected another two to three weeks of involvement.

🇮🇱 Israel
Strongly Supportive

Netanyahu characterized the operation as a joint effort and thanked Trump for his "historic leadership." Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu stated that after 20+ days, the regime's ballistic missile production and launch capabilities are no longer operational, and its uranium enrichment and nuclear weapon production infrastructure have been effectively destroyed.

🇬🇧 United Kingdom
Defensive Support Only

PM Keir Starmer, in a joint statement with leaders of France and Germany, condemned Iranian counter-strikes and called for a resumption of diplomacy, reiterating that Iran should end its nuclear programme and curtail its missile programme. Starmer said he did "not believe in regime change from the skies." The UK has not participated militarily in the strikes. UK aircraft have been deployed in a purely defensive role to intercept missiles in Qatar, Jordan, Iraq and Cyprus. Starmer said 35 countries signed a statement committing to work together on restoring maritime security in the Strait of Hormuz, with British Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper to lead a conference on the issue.

🇪🇺 France / Spain / Italy
Opposed — Restrictions Imposed

Spain, France and Italy have restricted US military operations, closing airspace, denying base access and limiting logistical support.

🇨🇳 China
Opposed — Pushing Ceasefire

China's delegate at the UN Security Council echoed the Secretary-General's call for urgent de-escalation, condemning the use or threat of force in international relations. China and Pakistan proposed a five-point plan including a ceasefire and reopening the Strait of Hormuz, as Beijing steps up diplomatic efforts. China has stayed out of the conflict militarily, but Beijing has concluded the war has shown the operational limits of the US-led coalition and serious restrictions on its hegemonic reach.

🇷🇺 Russia
Strongly Opposed

Russia's UN delegate said Iran had been "stabbed in the back" despite its willingness to engage in diplomacy, and that Washington's military buildup indicated the aggression was long planned. Russia drew parallels to the 2003 US claim of WMDs in Iraq.

🇦🇺 Australia
Limited Support — Not Consulted

Australian PM Albanese said Canberra hadn't rejected any requests from Trump for help, though Trump expressed disappointment in Australia's response. The Australian leader also pointed out the lack of consultation by the US and Israel with their allies before the attack on Iran, saying "Australia wasn't consulted before this action was undertaken."

🇵🇰 Pakistan
Mediating / Critical

Pakistan's UN delegate condemned "unwarranted attacks" against Iran, stating "Diplomacy has once again been derailed." Pakistan is simultaneously serving as a key backchannel intermediary, hosting secret meetings between Iranian and US-aligned envoys. Iran's former FM Kharazi was reportedly overseeing engagement with Pakistan for a possible meeting between Iranian officials and US Vice President JD Vance before he was injured in an airstrike on April 1.

🇸🇦🇦🇪🇶🇦 Gulf States (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain)
Under Attack — Defensive Posture

UAE Foreign Minister said the UAE would not be "blackmailed by terrorists." On 26 March 2026, the UAE along with Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, and Jordan issued a joint condemnation of Iran and its affiliated armed groups' attacks against countries in the region. As of 31 March 2026, the UAE alone has intercepted and destroyed 433 ballistic missiles, 1,977 drone attacks and 19 cruise missiles fired from Iran. Qatar's Emir and UAE President have held talks on the Iran war amid efforts to restore stability.

🇺🇳 United Nations
Strongly Critical

Military strikes on Iran and Iran's counter attacks on US army bases in Gulf countries threatened to ignite a wider war, delegates told an emergency Security Council meeting. China's delegate called it "shocking" that strikes came just as diplomatic negotiations were under way on Iran's nuclear programme. International human rights experts and UN officials said Trump's warning to strike power plants is an open threat to possibly commit a war crime. Pope Leo XIV expressed hope for an end to the Iran war ahead of Easter, calling for a reduction in violence and a renewed focus on diplomacy.

❓ What Remains Deeply Uncertain

Key uncertainties as of April 2, 2026 include: (1) The April 6 energy deadline: Trump set April 6 as the deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz or face US strikes on Iranian power plants. International human rights experts and UN officials say this threat is an open threat to possibly commit a war crime. (2) Ceasefire talks credibility gap: Iran has previously dismissed Trump's assertions of negotiations while confirming that some messages have been exchanged through intermediaries. Iran also does not have a "new president" — Masoud Pezeshkian has been president since 2024. Iranian officials have accused Washington of fabricating reports about diplomacy to manipulate the energy markets. (3) Ground invasion threat: The Pentagon is weighing sending up to 10,000 more U.S. troops to the Middle East. A ground war still seems unlikely: Iran's large size, population, and mountainous terrain make it a much tougher battlefield than Iraq, while current coalition troop numbers are clearly insufficient. (4) Nuclear reconstitution risk: For years, Iran maintained strategic ambiguity, staying just below the threshold of a bomb to avoid the very strikes that have now occurred. Tehran's new leadership faces a grim calculation: while the pursuit of nuclear weapons was dangerous, having an unfinished bomb was the fatal mistake. (5) New Supreme Leader's posture: RAND experts note that even with the selection of Mojtaba Khamenei as supreme leader, the Islamic Republic shaped by Ali Khamenei over 36 years cannot exist without him — the republic will be fundamentally altered, but is not over. (6) China's next move: Once the war ends, Beijing may decide to help Tehran rebuild militarily in return for oil.
